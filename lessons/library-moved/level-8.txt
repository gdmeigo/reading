The Library That Was Moved

At 3:10, the school library was on the second floor.

At 3:20, it was not.

Ken knew this because he had gone there to return a book about stars. He walked up the stairs, turned left, and stopped.

The library door was gone.

In its place was a plain wall and a poster that said:

PLEASE BE QUIET.
THE LIBRARY IS TRAVELING.

Mina read the poster and frowned. "Libraries do not travel."

"Buses travel," Ken said. "Maybe the library became a bus."

Mina gave him the look she used when science was being hurt.

They heard a soft beep from inside the wall.

Mika came with a notebook. "The door was seen here after lunch. It was used by three students. Then it was gone."

Taro ran up the stairs. "Good news. The library is downstairs."

"That is not good news," Mina said. "That is impossible news."

They ran downstairs. Next to the gym was the library door. The sign was the same. The smell of books was the same. But when Ken opened the door, the room inside was not the library.

It was the science room.

Inside, Aiko from the science club was standing beside a silver box. The box had a green light and a label:

TEMPORARY ROOM MOVER.

Mina took one step forward. "What is that?"

Aiko looked proud and tired. "It is a machine for open day. It can show visitors different rooms without making them walk."

"So the library was moved by this machine?" Mika asked.

"Only the door was moved," Aiko said. "The room should stay in place. The door should connect to it."

Taro looked into the science room. "It connected to the wrong room."

"Yes," Aiko said. "That is the problem."

The green light blinked. The door shook. When Ken opened it again, they saw the music room. A recorder rolled out and landed at Taro's feet.

"The library is becoming very musical," Taro said.

Mina checked the silver box. "The room names are written here: library, science room, music room, gym. Why is the library first?"

"Because books are important," Aiko said.

"No," Mina said. "Because the machine reads the first name when it is confused."

Mika looked at the poster. "The poster says the library is traveling. Who wrote that?"

Aiko raised her hand slowly. "I did. I thought it sounded fun."

"It sounded like a warning from a haunted book," Taro said.

The door beeped again. This time it opened into the library. Everyone saw shelves, desks, and the old reading lamp.

Ken stepped inside. "Good. We found it."

Then the door closed behind him.

Mina grabbed the handle. It did not move.

From the other side, Ken said, "I am fine. But the library is moving."

He was right. Through the small glass window, they saw the library change. First it was the library. Then it was the art room. Then it was the roof. Then it was the library again.

"The door is not moving rooms," Mina said. "It is changing which room it shows."

"How do we stop it?" Mika asked.

Aiko opened the machine cover. Inside were many wires and one small notebook. The notebook was written by Aiko, but one page was upside down.

Mina pointed. "This page was put in backward."

Aiko's face went red. "That page tells the machine how to come back."

Mika read the line. "If lost, return to the room with the quietest sound."

Everyone listened.

The gym was noisy. The music room was noisy. The science room was beeping. The library was quiet.

"Then the machine wants the library," Ken called from inside. "But it cannot find quiet because everyone is shouting."

Mina raised both hands. "Nobody speak."

The hall became silent. Even Taro held his breath, which was a school record.

The green light slowed.

Beep.

Beep.

Beep.

The door opened. Ken came out holding three books and one very confused recorder.

The machine was turned off by Aiko. The backward page was fixed by Mina. The poster was taken down by Mika.

At open day, Aiko changed the display. Visitors did not walk through moving doors. They looked at a small model instead.

Ken returned his star book at last.

The librarian stamped it and said, "You are late."

"The library was moved," Ken said.

The librarian looked at him over her glasses. "That is a new excuse."

Taro whispered, "A very quiet excuse."

Mina smiled. "And this time, it is scientifically possible."
