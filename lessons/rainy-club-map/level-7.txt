The Rainy Club Map

It rained on the day of the club festival. The soccer club could not play outside. The brass band could not play in the yard. Everyone stood in the hall and watched the rain run down the windows.

Miki had a map of the school. It was for visitors, but the rain made many arrows on it hard to read. "People cannot find the rooms," she said.

Ken was in the English club room. He studied the map, then read the old signs on the wall. "We can make a new map," he said. "But we must speak to every club first."

Miki and Ken walked through the school. They watched the cooking club make tea. They listened to the brass band practice in the music room. They saw the art club draw a big umbrella on the board.

In the library, an old woman from the town was standing with her grandson. She wanted to visit the calligraphy room, but the wet map showed only a black line. "I can read the room names," she said, "but I cannot read the arrows."

Miki took a blue marker from her pocket. "Then our map needs words, not only arrows."

At the end of the hall, they found the soccer club. The players looked sad. "We only have a ball," one boy said. "No one will visit us."

Ken looked at the wet floor. "Can you play a quiet game?" he asked.

The soccer club made a small paper goal. Visitors kicked a soft paper ball into it. Soon the hall was full of laughing voices.

Miki drew a new map and wrote one line under each room. Read a story. Drink tea. Watch music. Play paper soccer.

Ken put the map on a large board near the entrance. He did not use difficult words. He used words that small children and old visitors could read. The old woman came back first. She pointed at the board and said, "Now I can go."

At three o'clock, the rain stopped. But no one ran outside. They were still using Miki's map.

"The rain changed our festival," Miki said.

Ken smiled. "No. The map did."
