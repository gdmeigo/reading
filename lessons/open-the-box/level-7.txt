How to Open the Box

There is a black box on the library desk.

On the box is a card: "Do you know how to open me?"

Kaito looks for a key. Saki looks for a button. Ren shakes the box.

"Don't shake it," Saki says.

"I want to know what is inside," Ren says.

The box has four pictures: a book, a cup, a clock, and a star. Under the pictures are four small holes.

Kaito finds another card in a book. It says, "Read first."

Saki opens the book. There is a story about a boy. He cannot open a door because he does not listen.

"Maybe we have to read the story," she says.

Ren reads the first line. "The boy drinks water."

Kaito puts a pencil in the hole under the cup. The box clicks.

Saki reads the next line. "He watches the clock."

Ren puts a pencil in the clock hole. Another click.

Kaito reads. "He reads a book under a star."

Saki puts pencils in the book hole and the star hole.

The box opens.

Inside is not money. It is not candy. It is a small sign: "Good readers open doors."

Ren is disappointed for one second. Then he sees three cookies under the sign.

"Good readers also eat cookies," he says.

Mr. Sato comes from behind the shelf. "Only after they put the pencils back."

Kaito laughs. "Now we know how to open the box."

Saki says, "Read first."

Ren says, "And do not shake it."
