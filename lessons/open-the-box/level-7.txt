How to Open the Box

There is a black box on the library desk.

On the box is a card: "Do you know how to open me?"

Ken looks for a key. Mika looks for a button. Taro shakes the box.

"Don't shake it," Mika says.

"I want to know what is inside," Taro says.

The box has four pictures: a book, a cup, a clock, and a star. Under the pictures are four small holes.

Ken finds another card in a book. It says, "Read first."

Mika opens the book. There is a story about a boy who cannot open a door because he does not listen.

"Maybe we have to read the story," she says.

Taro reads the first line. "The boy drinks water."

Ken puts a pencil in the hole under the cup. The box clicks.

Mika reads the next line. "He watches the clock."

Taro puts a pencil in the clock hole. Another click.

Ken reads. "He reads a book under a star."

Mika puts pencils in the book hole and the star hole.

The box opens.

Inside is not money. It is not candy. It is a small sign: "Good readers open doors."

Taro is disappointed for one second. Then he sees three cookies under the sign.

"Good readers also eat cookies," he says.

Mr. Sato comes from behind the shelf. "Only after they put the pencils back."

Ken laughs. "Now we know how to open the box."

Mika says, "Read first."

Taro says, "And do not shake it."
