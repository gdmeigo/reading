The Cake Was Moved

Miki brought a cake to the club room. It was for Ken's birthday. The cake was small, but it had a big sign on it.

The sign said, "Do not eat."

Ken read the sign and smiled. "That is clear."

"Very clear," Miki said. "I will get plates. Please watch the cake."

Ken watched it. Then the door opened. Jaio came in with Suneta behind him.

"Nice cake," Jaio said.

"It is not for you," Ken said.

"I know," Jaio said. "I am only looking."

He looked from the left. He looked from the right. Suneta looked from very near.

Ken pointed at the sign. "Do not eat."

"I can read," Jaio said.

Miki came back with plates. The cake was not on the desk.

Everyone was silent.

"The cake was moved," Ken said.

Miki looked at Jaio. Jaio looked at Suneta. Suneta looked at the floor.

"It was moved by the wind," Jaio said.

"There is no wind in this room," Miki said.

"Then it was moved by a very hungry wind," Suneta said.

Ken saw a line of cream on the desk. It went to the shelf. On the shelf, the cake was under a bowl.

"Why is the cake there?" Ken asked.

Jaio raised his hand. "I moved it."

Miki folded her arms.

Jaio spoke quickly. "But I did not eat it. I moved it because Suneta was looking too near."

Suneta nodded. "My nose was in danger."

Miki opened the bowl. The cake was safe. Only one strawberry was missing.

Everyone looked at Suneta.

Suneta put a red paper circle on the cake. "It is still a strawberry."

Ken laughed. "A paper strawberry?"

"A birthday strawberry," Suneta said.

Miki cut the cake. Jaio got the smallest piece. Suneta got the piece with the paper strawberry.

Ken put the sign on the wall: "The cake was moved, but the party was saved."
