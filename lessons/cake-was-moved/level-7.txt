The Cake Was Moved

Hana brought a cake to the club room. It was for Taro's birthday. The cake was small, but it had a big sign on it.

The sign said, "Do not eat."

Taro read the sign and smiled. "That is clear."

"Very clear," Hana said. "I will get plates. Please watch the cake."

Taro watched it. Then the door opened. Jaio came in with Suneta behind him.

"Nice cake," Jaio said.

"It is not for you," Taro said.

"I know," Jaio said. "I am only looking."

He looked from the left. He looked from the right. Suneta looked from very near.

Taro pointed at the sign. "Do not eat."

"I can read," Jaio said.

Hana came back with plates. The cake was not on the desk.

Everyone was silent.

"The cake was moved," Taro said.

Hana looked at Jaio. Jaio looked at Suneta. Suneta looked at the floor.

"It was moved by the wind," Jaio said.

"There is no wind in this room," Hana said.

"Then it was moved by a very hungry wind," Suneta said.

Taro saw a line of cream on the desk. It went to the shelf. On the shelf, the cake was under a bowl.

"Why is the cake there?" Taro asked.

Jaio raised his hand. "I moved it."

Hana folded her arms.

Jaio spoke quickly. "But I did not eat it. I moved it because Suneta was looking too near."

Suneta nodded. "My nose was in danger."

Hana opened the bowl. The cake was safe. Only one strawberry was missing.

Everyone looked at Suneta.

Suneta put a red paper circle on the cake. "It is still a strawberry."

Taro laughed. "A paper strawberry?"

"A birthday strawberry," Suneta said.

Hana cut the cake. Jaio got the smallest piece. Suneta got the piece with the paper strawberry.

Taro put the sign on the wall: "The cake was moved, but the party was saved."
