More Than One Moon

On Monday night, Ken saw the moon from the classroom window.
Then he saw another moon in the hall mirror.

"There is more than one moon," Taro said.

Mika shook her head.
"There is only one real moon."

But the mirror moon was strange.
It had a black dot on the left side.
The real moon did not.

The next night, the black dot was bigger.
Ken wrote it in his notebook.
On Wednesday, the dot looked like a small door.

Mika checked the mirror.
There was nothing behind it.
Taro looked outside.
There was no cloud and no balloon.

Then Yui came with an old photo.
In the photo, the same mirror was in the science room.
On its glass was a paper moon with a black door.

"Maybe the mirror remembers the old room," Yui said.

Ken turned off the hall light.
The mirror moon became bright.
The black door opened.

Inside the mirror, they saw a small boy putting a note under the frame.

Ken touched the frame.
A yellow note fell out.

It said, "If you see more than one moon, please clean the mirror."

Everyone was quiet for a moment.
Then Taro laughed first.

Mika cleaned the mirror with a cloth.
The second moon disappeared.

Ken looked at the real moon outside.
"Mystery solved," he said.

Yui smiled.
"And the mirror is finally clean."
