The Folded Map Race

The town race was supposed to be easy.

Each team got a map. Each map showed five checkpoints: the bridge, the bakery, the park, the station, and the old clock tower. The first team to visit all five places and return to school would win.

Miki loved maps, so she was made the leader of her team. Ren carried the water. Taro carried snacks. Ken carried the team card because Mika said he was least likely to eat it.

Jaio's team stood beside them. Jaio held his map upside down. Suneta held a red flag and looked worried.

"We will win," Jaio said. "My map-reading power is great."

Miki glanced at his map. "The river is at the top."

"I know," Jaio said. He turned the map around. "I was testing it."

The race started at nine.

Miki led her team to the bridge first. The card was stamped by a volunteer. Then they ran to the bakery. The smell of bread almost defeated Taro, but Ren pulled him away.

"Snacks later," Ren said.

At the park, they saw Jaio's team running in circles around the fountain.

"The map says the station is next," Suneta said.

"Then why are we at the fountain?" Jaio asked.

"Because you said the fountain looked like a station."

Miki wanted to help, but Jaio waved them away.

"Do not teach your rivals," he said.

At the station, something strange happened. The station checkpoint was not there. There was only a sign:

CHECKPOINT MOVED.
FOLLOW THE FOLDED ROAD.

"Folded road?" Ken asked.

Miki opened the map again. One corner had been folded under. When she unfolded it, a thin blue line appeared. It was not printed on the front of the map. It was printed on the back, and it matched the roads only when the paper was folded.

"This map was folded by the race committee," Miki said.

"On purpose?" Taro asked.

"Maybe," Mika said. "Or maybe someone made a mistake."

They followed the blue line. It led away from the station and toward a narrow street behind the library.

There they found the station checkpoint. It was a small desk with Mr. Sato sitting behind it.

"Good," he said. "You found the folded road."

"Was the checkpoint moved by you?" Miki asked.

"It was moved by the committee," Mr. Sato said. "We wanted teams to read carefully."

Taro looked proud. "I read the snack bag carefully."

They got the station stamp and ran toward the clock tower. But halfway there, they saw Suneta sitting on a low wall. Jaio was standing beside him, trying to look angry and not worried.

"What happened?" Ren asked.

Suneta held up their map. It was torn in the middle.

"It was not torn by me," Jaio said quickly.

Suneta sighed. "It was torn when Jaio tried to fold it with power."

Jaio looked away. "The map was weak."

Miki took the torn pieces. The blue line was broken, but the front of the map still showed the streets.

"We can help," she said.

"No," Jaio said. "We are rivals."

Mika looked at the clock. "If we leave them, they may not finish."

Taro whispered, "Also, if they stay lost, they will eat all my snacks."

Miki put the map pieces on the wall. She showed Suneta how the roads connected. Ren used tape from his bag to fix the tear. Ken copied the last checkpoint mark onto Jaio's team card.

"There," Miki said. "Now you can finish."

Jaio looked at the fixed map. "Why help us?"

"Because a race is better when everyone reaches the end," Miki said.

For once, Jaio did not have a loud answer.

Both teams ran to the clock tower. The final stamp was given by the town mayor, who looked very serious for a person holding a purple stamp shaped like a fish.

Then the teams raced back to school.

Miki's team arrived first. Jaio's team arrived one minute later. Jaio was breathing hard. Suneta was carrying the red flag like a tired soldier.

The teacher checked the cards. "Miki's team has all five stamps. Jaio's team also has all five stamps."

Jaio lifted his head. "Then we are second."

"Yes," the teacher said. "But there is a special prize today."

She held up a small ribbon. It said: BEST MAP HELPERS.

"This prize is given to the team that helped another team finish."

The ribbon was given to Miki's team. Jaio stared at it. Then he slowly clapped. Suneta clapped faster because he was relieved the race was over.

At lunch, Taro opened the snack bag at last. Inside was only one cookie.

"Who ate them?" he cried.

Ren pointed to the bag. "Read carefully."

On the bag was a small note:

SNACKS WERE MOVED TO THE FINISH TABLE.

Everyone ran to the table. There were enough cookies for both teams.

Jaio took one and held it up. "I found the final checkpoint."

Miki smiled. "Only because it was not folded."
