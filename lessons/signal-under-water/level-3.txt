The Signal Under Water

These pens are on the science desk.
One pen is red, and one pen is blue.

Ken puts the blue pen by a glass of water.
The water begins to shine.

"That is not normal water," Mika says.

A small light moves under the water.
It makes three dots, then one line.

Ken holds the red pen near the glass.
The light follows it.

"Maybe the disk is reading the color," Mika says.

Taro writes the dots on paper.
The dots make a small map of the classroom.

Under the teacher's desk, they find a tiny silver disk.
It is warm.

The disk opens, and a voice says, "Thank you for finding my signal."

Ken looks at the pens again.
They are normal pens.
The disk was watching red and blue.

"These pens are just pens," he says. "But the disk used them."
