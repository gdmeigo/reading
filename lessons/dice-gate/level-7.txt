The Dice Gate

Ken, Miki, and Yui were in the English room after school. On the desk was an old map, a small box, and one white die.

"This is not a game," Miki said.

"It looks like a game," Ken said.

Yui opened the box. Inside it was a card. The card said, "Find how to open the gate. Use the die, the map, and one brave voice."

Ken looked at the map. It showed the school, but the school had a forest behind the music room and a mountain under the library.

"Our school does not have a mountain," Ken said.

"Maybe it does today," Yui said.

Miki put the die on the map. It moved by itself and stopped on the music room.

The three students went there. The room was dark. A paper gate was on the wall. It had six small circles.

"We have to roll the die," Ken said.

He rolled it. Six.

The paper gate opened a little. A cold wind came out. Behind the paper was not the wall. It was a stone path.

Yui smiled. "This is better than cleaning duty."

They stepped through the gate. The stone path led to a small bridge. Under the bridge, a voice said, "Who goes there?"

Ken looked down. A tiny guard stood there with a spoon for a sword.

"We are students," Miki said.

"Students cannot pass," said the guard. "Only heroes can pass."

Yui thought for a moment. Then she held up her English notebook. "A hero studies before a test."

The guard looked at the notebook. "Strong magic."

He moved away.

At the end of the path was a door with no handle. On it were the words: "Show one brave voice."

Ken took a deep breath. "Can we go home after this?"

The door opened at once.

Miki laughed. "That was your brave voice?"

"Very brave," Ken said. "I asked the question everyone wanted to ask."

Behind the door was the English room. The map was now normal. The die was still on the desk, but the card had new words.

"Good party," it said. "Next time, bring snacks."

Yui closed the box. "Next time?"

Ken put the box in the shelf. "First, we learn how to say no to magic homework."
