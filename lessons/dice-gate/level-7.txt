The Dice Gate

Yuto, Iori, and Noa were in the English room after school. On the desk was an old map, a small box, and one white die.

"This is not a game," Iori said.

"It looks like a game," Yuto said.

Noa opened the box. Inside it was a card. The card said, "Find how to open the gate. Use the die, the map, and one brave voice."

Yuto looked at the map. It showed the school, but the school had a forest behind the music room and a mountain under the library.

"Our school does not have a mountain," Yuto said.

"Maybe it does today," Noa said.

Iori put the die on the map. It moved by itself and stopped on the music room.

The three students went there. The room was dark. A paper gate was on the wall. It had six small circles.

"We have to roll the die," Yuto said.

He rolled it. Six.

The paper gate opened a little. A cold wind came out. Behind the paper was not the wall. It was a stone path.

Noa smiled. "This is better than cleaning duty."

They stepped through the gate. The stone path led to a small bridge. Under the bridge, a voice said, "Who goes there?"

Yuto looked down. A tiny guard stood there with a spoon for a sword.

"We are students," Iori said.

"Students cannot pass," said the guard. "Only heroes can pass."

Noa thought for a moment. Then she held up her English notebook. "A hero studies before a test."

The guard looked at the notebook. "Strong magic."

He moved away.

At the end of the path was a door with no handle. On it were the words: "Show one brave voice."

Yuto took a deep breath. "Can we go home after this?"

The door opened at once.

Iori laughed. "That was your brave voice?"

"Very brave," Yuto said. "I asked the question everyone wanted to ask."

Behind the door was the English room. The map was now normal. The die was still on the desk, but the card had new words.

"Good party," it said. "Next time, bring snacks."

Noa closed the box. "Next time?"

Yuto put the box in the shelf. "First, we learn how to say no to magic homework."
