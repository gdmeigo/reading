The Gift Lesson

Mr. Tanaka wanted to teach the class an important lesson about giving. He put three bags on the desk.

"I will give this red bag to Taro," he said. "Taro, show Hana what is inside."

Taro opened the red bag. There was one potato.

The class was quiet.

"Thank you," Taro said slowly.

Mr. Tanaka gave the blue bag to Hana. "Hana, show Jiro."

Hana opened it. There were two potatoes.

Jiro got the yellow bag. He opened it before Mr. Tanaka could speak. There were three potatoes.

"Is this math?" Jiro asked.

"No," said Mr. Tanaka. "It is kindness."

"Then we have to give the potatoes to someone," Hana said.

No one understood. Mr. Tanaka looked worried. He had planned a beautiful lesson, but now the class was only looking at potatoes.

Then the door opened. Ms. Kato from the cooking room came in. "Where are my potatoes?"

Mr. Tanaka's face went red. He looked at the bags. He looked at the class.

Taro raised his hand. "Maybe today's lesson is about giving potatoes back."

Everyone laughed, even Mr. Tanaka.

The class took the bags to the cooking room. Taro gave Ms. Kato the red bag. Hana gave her the blue bag. Jiro gave her the yellow bag and said, "I can teach you something. Three is more than two."

The potatoes were washed by the class. Then they were cut by Ms. Kato because the knives were sharp.

Ms. Kato smiled. "Then I will teach you something, too."

She showed the class how to make potato soup. At the end of the day, everyone had a cup.

Mr. Tanaka wrote on the board: "A good lesson can change."

The class copied the sentence into their notebooks.

Taro drank his soup. "And a good gift can come back as lunch."
