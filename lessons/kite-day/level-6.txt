The Paper Rocket

Ren came to school with a paper rocket in a long box. It was
show day, and every student had to show something to the class.
Ren had worked on his rocket for three nights. At home, it went
up very well. At school, he wanted it to fly higher than the
teacher's desk.

Before class, Miki saw Ren looking into the box.
"Are you nervous?" she asked.
"A little," said Ren. "It worked at home, but this room is bigger."

When Ren's turn came, he put the rocket on a small pump. He
looked at the class, took a breath, and pushed the pump hard.

The rocket jumped, turned, and fell onto the floor.

No one laughed. That made Ren feel worse. He picked up the
rocket and said, "It worked at home."

Miki came to the front and looked at the bottom of the rocket.
There was a small hole in the paper.
"The air is going out here," she said.

Ren did not have tape. Miki had thin tape in her pencil case. Daichi
had strong tape in his bag. Saki had scissors. Mr. Sato gave them
one minute. They fixed the rocket together.

Ren tried again. This time, he did not push too hard. The rocket
went up, over the first row, and onto Mr. Sato's open book.

For one second, the room was quiet.

Then Mr. Sato looked at the rocket standing between two pages.
"Now I have a rocket bookmark," he said.

Everyone laughed and clapped. Ren bowed, but he was laughing,
too.

After class, Ren wrote one sentence on the rocket:
"This rocket flies better with friends."
