The Paper Rocket

Daichi came to school with a paper rocket in a long box. It was
show day, and every student had to show something to the class.
Daichi had worked on his rocket for three nights. At home, it went
up very well. At school, he wanted it to fly higher than the
teacher's desk.

Before class, Aya saw Daichi looking into the box.
"Are you nervous?" she asked.
"A little," said Daichi. "It worked at home, but this room is bigger."

When Daichi's turn came, he put the rocket on a small pump. He
looked at the class, took a breath, and pushed the pump hard.

The rocket jumped, turned, and fell onto the floor.

No one laughed. That made Daichi feel worse. He picked up the
rocket and said, "It worked at home."

Aya came to the front and looked at the bottom of the rocket.
There was a small hole in the paper.
"The air is going out here," she said.

Daichi did not have tape. Aya had thin tape in her pencil case. Daichi
had strong tape in his bag. Aya had scissors. Mr. Sato gave them
one minute. They fixed the rocket together.

Daichi tried again. This time, he did not push too hard. The rocket
went up, over the first row, and onto Mr. Sato's open book.

For one second, the room was quiet.

Then Mr. Sato looked at the rocket standing between two pages.
"Now I have a rocket bookmark," he said.

Everyone laughed and clapped. Daichi bowed, but he was laughing,
too.

After class, Daichi wrote one sentence on the rocket:
"This rocket flies better with friends."
