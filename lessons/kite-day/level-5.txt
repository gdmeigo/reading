The Paper Rocket

Ren came to school with a paper rocket in a long box.
It was show day, and every student had to show something.
Ren wanted his rocket to go higher than the teacher's desk.

Before class, Miki saw Ren looking at the rocket.
"Are you nervous?" she asked.
"A little," said Ren. "It worked at home."

When Ren's turn came, he put the rocket on a small pump.
He pushed the pump hard.
The rocket jumped, turned, and fell onto the floor.

No one said anything.
Ren's face got red.

Miki picked up the rocket and looked at the bottom.
There was a small hole in the paper.
"The air is going out here," she said.

Ren did not have tape.
Miki had tape, but it was very thin.
Daichi had strong tape.
Saki had scissors.
They fixed the rocket together.

Ren tried again.
He pushed the pump.
The rocket went up, over the first row, and onto Mr. Sato's book.

Mr. Sato opened the book.
There was the rocket, standing between two pages.

"Now I have a rocket bookmark," he said.

The class laughed.
Ren bowed.
It was not the show he planned, but it was the show everyone remembered.
