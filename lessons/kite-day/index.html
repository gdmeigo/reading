The Paper Rocket

Five graded text-only readings.

Level 1

Assumed grading: present have/see/put/go/fall/try, can, simple nouns.

Ren has a paper rocket.

Miki sees the rocket.

It is big.

It is red.

Ren puts it on the desk.

"It can go up," he says.

The rocket does not go up.

It falls.

Ren is sad.

Miki sees a small hole.

She puts tape on the hole.

Ren tries again.

The rocket goes up.

The class says, "Wow!"

Ren smiles.

Level 2

Assumed grading: Level 1 plus came/asked/said/pushed/went, did not, happy way.

Ren came to school with a paper rocket.

Today was show day.

He put the rocket on his desk.

Miki came to Ren's desk.

"Is that your rocket?" she asked.

"Yes," said Ren. "It can go up."

Ren put the rocket on a pump.

He pushed the pump.

The rocket went up a little.

Then it came down.

Some boys laughed.

Ren did not laugh.

Miki looked at the rocket.

There was a small hole in it.

She put tape on the hole.

Ren pushed the pump again.

The rocket went up.

It went over the desks.

This time, everyone laughed in a happy way.

Level 3

Assumed grading: Level 2 plus wanted, front row, carefully, air, landing, clapped.

Ren came into the classroom with a paper rocket.

It was show day, and Ren was first.

He wanted the rocket to go over the desks.

Miki sat in the front row.

"Good luck," she said.

Ren put the rocket on a small pump.

He pushed the pump.

The rocket went up, but only a little.

Then it fell onto the floor.

The room became quiet.

Ren picked up the rocket.

"It worked at home," he said.

Miki looked at the rocket carefully.

There was a small hole near the bottom.

"Air is going out," she said.

Ren had no tape.

Miki had tape in her pencil case.

She put tape on the hole.

Ren tried again.

This time, the rocket went up and over the first desk.

Then it came down on Mr. Sato's book.

Mr. Sato looked at the rocket.

Then he smiled.

"Good landing," he said.

The class clapped.

Ren smiled, too.

Level 4

Assumed grading: Level 3 plus had to, higher than, nervous, together, remembered.

Ren came to school with a paper rocket in a long box.

It was show day, and every student had to show something.

Ren wanted his rocket to go higher than the teacher's desk.

Before class, Miki saw Ren looking at the rocket.

"Are you nervous?" she asked.

"A little," said Ren. "It worked at home."

When Ren's turn came, he put the rocket on a small pump.

He pushed the pump hard.

The rocket jumped, turned, and fell onto the floor.

No one said anything.

Ren's face got red.

Miki picked up the rocket and looked at the bottom.

There was a small hole in the paper.

"The air is going out here," she said.

Ren did not have tape.

Miki had tape, but it was very thin.

Daichi had strong tape.

Saki had scissors.

They fixed the rocket together.

Ren tried again.

He pushed the pump.

The rocket went up, over the first row, and onto Mr. Sato's book.

Mr. Sato opened the book.

There was the rocket, standing between two pages.

"Now I have a rocket bookmark," he said.

The class laughed.

Ren bowed.

It was not the show he planned, but it was the show everyone remembered.

Level 5

Assumed grading: Level 4 plus longer paragraphs, for three nights, took a breath, teamwork theme.

Ren came to school with a paper rocket in a long box. It was show day, and every student had to show something to the class. Ren had worked on his rocket for three nights. At home, it went up very well. At school, he wanted it to fly higher than the teacher's desk.

Before class, Miki saw Ren looking into the box. "Are you nervous?" she asked. "A little," said Ren. "It worked at home, but this room is bigger."

When Ren's turn came, he put the rocket on a small pump. He looked at the class, took a breath, and pushed the pump hard.

The rocket jumped, turned, and fell onto the floor.

No one laughed. That made Ren feel worse. He picked up the rocket and said, "It worked at home."

Miki came to the front and looked at the bottom of the rocket. There was a small hole in the paper. "The air is going out here," she said.

Ren did not have tape. Miki had thin tape in her pencil case. Daichi had strong tape in his bag. Saki had scissors. Mr. Sato gave them one minute. They fixed the rocket together.

Ren tried again. This time, he did not push too hard. The rocket went up, over the first row, and onto Mr. Sato's open book.

For one second, the room was quiet.

Then Mr. Sato looked at the rocket standing between two pages. "Now I have a rocket bookmark," he said.

Everyone laughed and clapped. Ren bowed, but he was laughing, too.

After class, Ren wrote one sentence on the rocket: "This rocket flies better with friends."
