Because of the Soup

The cooking room smells good. Kaito, Saki, and Gaku are making soup for the class party.

Kaito cuts carrots. Saki washes cups. Gaku reads the recipe.

"Put in one spoon of salt," Gaku says.

Kaito puts in one spoon.

Five minutes later, Saki tastes the soup. Her eyes get big.

"It is very salty."

Gaku looks at the recipe. "Because Kaito put in salt."

Kaito says, "No. It is salty because you said one spoon."

Saki looks at the table. There are two white spoons. One is small. One is very big.

"Which spoon did you mean?" she asks.

Gaku points at the small spoon. Kaito points at the big spoon.

The soup is almost a sea.

They do not have time to start again. The class party begins in thirty minutes.

Saki thinks. "We need more water and more vegetables."

Kaito gets water. Gaku gets potatoes. Saki adds corn. The pot is now very full.

Mr. Sato comes in. "Why is there so much soup?"

Gaku says, "Because of the spoon."

Kaito says, "Because of Gaku."

Saki says, "Because of both."

At the party, everyone eats the soup. It is not too salty. It is warm and full of vegetables.

Nana comes for a second cup. Then a third cup.

"This soup is great," she says. "Why is there so much?"

Kaito, Saki, and Gaku look at each other.

Gaku smiles. "Because we planned it."

Saki laughs. "No. Because of the soup."
