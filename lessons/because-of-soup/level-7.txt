Because of the Soup

The cooking room smells good. Ken, Mika, and Taro are making soup for the class party.

Ken cuts carrots. Mika washes cups. Taro reads the recipe.

"Put in one spoon of salt," Taro says.

Ken puts in one spoon.

Five minutes later, Mika tastes the soup. Her eyes get big.

"It is very salty."

Taro looks at the recipe. "Because Ken put in salt."

Ken says, "No. It is salty because you said one spoon."

Mika looks at the table. There are two white spoons. One is small. One is very big.

"Which spoon did you mean?" she asks.

Taro points at the small spoon. Ken points at the big spoon.

The soup is almost a sea.

They do not have time to start again. The class party begins in thirty minutes.

Mika thinks. "We need more water and more vegetables."

Ken gets water. Taro gets potatoes. Mika adds corn. The pot is now very full.

Mr. Sato comes in. "Why is there so much soup?"

Taro says, "Because of the spoon."

Ken says, "Because of Taro."

Mika says, "Because of both."

At the party, everyone eats the soup. It is not too salty. It is warm and full of vegetables.

Yui comes for a second cup. Then a third cup.

"This soup is great," she says. "Why is there so much?"

Ken, Mika, and Taro look at each other.

Taro smiles. "Because we planned it."

Mika laughs. "No. Because of the soup."
