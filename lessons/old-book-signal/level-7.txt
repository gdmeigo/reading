The Old Book Signal

This book is old. This book is brown. This book is on the top shelf.

Ken wants the book, but the shelf is high. Mika brings a chair.

"This chair is not strong," Ken says.

"Then don't stand on it," Mika says. "Look at the book."

Ken looks. A small white card is in the book. The card is not a page. It is a sign.

Taro comes into the library. "That book is mine," he says.

"No," Mika says. "This book is a library book."

Taro looks at the card and gets quiet. On the card is one word: "BELL."

Ken says, "This is strange."

Mika opens the book on the table. There is no bell in it. There is a picture of a school. In the picture, a boy is under a tree. The boy has a bell.

"This boy is you," Mika says to Taro.

Taro laughs. "No. That boy is my father. This book is from my house. I gave it to the library."

"Then why is the card here?" Ken asks.

Taro turns the last page. There is a small pocket. In the pocket is a key.

Mika points at a little box near the desk. "This box is old, too."

Ken opens the box. There is a bell inside. It is small and silver.

Taro smiles. "My father was on the library team. The bell is for the first library day."

Ken rings the bell one time. It is soft, but everyone looks up.

Mika says, "This book is not only old."

Ken nods. "This book is a signal."
