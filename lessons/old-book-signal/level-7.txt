The Old Book Signal

This book is old. This book is brown. This book is on the top shelf.

Kaito wants the book, but the shelf is high. Saki brings a seat.

"This seat is not strong," Kaito says.

"Then don't stand on it," Saki says. "Look at the book."

Kaito looks. A small white card is in the book. The card is not a page. It is a sign.

Ren comes into the library. "That book is mine," he says.

"No," Saki says. "This book is a library book."

Ren looks at the card and gets quiet. On the card is one word: "BELL."

Kaito says, "This is strange."

Saki opens the book on the table. There is no bell in it. There is a picture of a school. In the picture, a boy is under a tree. The boy has a bell.

"This boy is you," Saki says to Ren.

Ren laughs. "No. That boy is my father. This book is from my house. I gave it to the library."

"Then why is the card here?" Kaito asks.

Ren turns the last page. There is a small pocket. In the pocket is a key.

Saki points at a little box near the desk. "This box is old, too."

Kaito opens the box. There is a bell inside. It is small and silver.

Ren smiles. "My father was on the library team. The bell is for the first library day."

Kaito rings the bell one time. It is soft, but everyone looks up.

Saki says, "This book is not only old."

Kaito nods. "This book is a signal."
