The Blue Lunch Box

Daichi came to school before class on a cold morning. He put his
bag by his desk and went to the shoe boxes. On the bench there
was a blue lunch box. It was shut. It had no name. Daichi knew it
was not his lunch box, because his was green.

Emi came in after him. She saw Daichi standing by the bench.
"What are you looking at?" she asked. Daichi pointed at the lunch
box. "Someone left it here," he said. "Maybe there is lunch in
it." Emi looked around, but no student was near the bench.

Daichi opened the lunch box slowly. There was no rice in it. There
was no egg in it. There was a small towel, a white ball, and a
note. Emi took the note and read it aloud.

"Please help my friend. She is cold. I will come back."

The white ball moved under the towel.

Daichi shut the lunch box at once. "We need a teacher," he said.
Emi put her hand on the cover. "Wait," she said. "It may need
air." Daichi opened the box again. The white ball came out of the
towel. It was not a ball. It was a very small bird.

The bird did not fly. It only looked at Daichi and Emi. Daichi put the
towel around the bird. Emi kept the lunch box open. The bird got
quiet. Daichi said, "Who put a bird in a lunch box?" Emi looked at
the note again. "Someone who wanted to help it," she said.

Then a girl ran to the shoe boxes. She had a small cup of water
in one hand. She saw the open lunch box and stopped.
"That is my lunch box," she said. "And that is my bird."

Her name was Yuna. She found the bird on the ground near the
school gate. The bird was cold, and Yuna did not know what to
do. She put it in her lunch box with her towel. Then she went to
get water. She came back as fast as she could.

Daichi gave the bird to Yuna. Emi gave her the note. Yuna smiled
and said, "Thank you for not leaving her alone."

At that time, Mr. Sato came to the shoe boxes. He looked at the
bird and then at the three students. "The bird needs a warm
place," he said. "Let's take her to the teachers' room."

Yuna held the bird carefully. Daichi carried the blue lunch box.
Emi carried the small cup.

The lunch box was empty again, but Yuna was smiling.

After school, Yuna came to Daichi and Emi.
"The bird can fly now," she said.

Daichi looked at the blue lunch box in Yuna's hand.
"So it was a lunch box again?" he asked.

Yuna opened it.
Inside were three small cookies.

"Today," she said, "it is a thank-you box."
