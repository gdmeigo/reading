The Blue Lunch Box

Daichi came to school before class.
It was a cold morning.
He saw a blue lunch box on the bench by the shoe boxes.
The lunch box had no name.
It was shut.

Emi came to the bench and saw Daichi.
"What are you looking at?" she asked.
"This lunch box," said Daichi. "It is not mine."

Emi looked around.
No student was near the bench.
Daichi opened the lunch box slowly.
There was no lunch in it.
There was a small towel, a note, and a white ball.

Emi read the note.
"Please help my friend. She is cold. I will come back."

The white ball moved under the towel.
Daichi shut the lunch box.
"Maybe we should call a teacher," he said.
"Wait," said Emi. "Look."

The white ball came out of the towel.
It was a small bird.
The bird did not fly.
It only looked at Daichi.

Daichi put the towel around the bird.
Emi kept the lunch box open.
The bird got quiet.

Then a girl ran to the shoe boxes.
She had a bag in one hand and a small cup in the other.
"My bird is in that box," she said.
"I put her there because she was cold."

Daichi gave the bird to the girl.
Emi gave her the note.
The girl smiled.
"I was getting water for her," she said.

At that time, the teacher came.
The teacher looked at the bird and said,
"Good work, Daichi and Emi."

The girl put the bird in her hands.
The bird made a small sound.
It was a happy sound.
