The Blue Lunch Box

Five graded text-only readings.

Level 1

Assumed grading: present be/have/see/open/say, it/this, in/on, not, simple nouns.

Ken sees a blue lunch box.

It is on the bench.

It is not Ken's lunch box.

Yui sees the lunch box, too.

"Is it your lunch box?" she asks.

"No," says Ken.

Ken opens the lunch box.

There is no lunch in it.

There is a small note.

The note says, "Please help."

Ken looks at Yui.

Yui looks at Ken.

Then the lunch box moves.

Level 2

Assumed grading: Level 1 plus basic past forms, asked/said/saw/opened/took/shut, did not.

Ken came to school early.

He saw a blue lunch box on the bench.

It was not his lunch box.

Yui came to the bench.

"Is that your lunch box?" she asked.

"No," said Ken. "I don't know it."

Ken opened the lunch box.

There was no rice in it.

There was no egg in it.

There was only a small note.

Yui took the note.

The note said, "Please help my friend."

Then the lunch box moved.

Ken shut it.

Yui said, "Don't shut it."

Ken opened it again.

There was a small white ball in it.

The ball moved.

"It is not a ball," said Yui.

Level 3

Assumed grading: Level 2 plus before/by, there was, carefully, gave, warm/cold, simple reveal.

Ken came to school before class.

He saw a blue lunch box on the bench by the shoe boxes.

There was no name on it.

Yui came to the bench.

"Is it yours?" she asked.

"No," said Ken. "My lunch box is green."

Ken opened the blue lunch box.

There was no lunch in it.

There was a small note and a white ball.

Yui read the note.

"Please help my friend. She is cold."

The white ball moved.

Ken moved back.

Yui looked at it carefully.

"It is not a ball," she said.

"It is a small bird."

The bird was very small.

It did not fly.

Ken put his hand near the bird.

The bird came into his hand.

Then a girl came to the shoe boxes.

She was looking for something.

She looked at the bench.

She looked at Ken's hand.

"My bird!" she said.

Ken gave the bird to the girl.

The girl smiled.

"Thank you," she said.

The blue lunch box was hers.

It was not a lunch box today.

It was a small warm house.

Level 4

Assumed grading: Level 3 plus cold morning, around, because, maybe, should, simple teacher resolution.

Ken came to school before class.

It was a cold morning.

He saw a blue lunch box on the bench by the shoe boxes.

The lunch box had no name.

It was shut.

Yui came to the bench and saw Ken.

"What are you looking at?" she asked.

"This lunch box," said Ken. "It is not mine."

Yui looked around.

No student was near the bench.

Ken opened the lunch box slowly.

There was no lunch in it.

There was a small towel, a note, and a white ball.

Yui read the note.

"Please help my friend. She is cold. I will come back."

The white ball moved under the towel.

Ken shut the lunch box.

"Maybe we should call a teacher," he said.

"Wait," said Yui. "Look."

The white ball came out of the towel.

It was a small bird.

The bird did not fly.

It only looked at Ken.

Ken put the towel around the bird.

Yui kept the lunch box open.

The bird got quiet.

Then a girl ran to the shoe boxes.

She had a bag in one hand and a small cup in the other.

"My bird is in that box," she said.

"I put her there because she was cold."

Ken gave the bird to the girl.

Yui gave her the note.

The girl smiled.

"I was getting water for her," she said.

At that time, the teacher came.

The teacher looked at the bird and said,

"Good work, Ken and Yui."

The girl put the bird in her hands.

The bird made a small sound.

It was a happy sound.

Level 5

Assumed grading: Level 4 plus longer paragraphs, because clauses, careful sequence, named third character, final callback.

Ken came to school before class on a cold morning. He put his bag by his desk and went to the shoe boxes. On the bench there was a blue lunch box. It was shut. It had no name. Ken knew it was not his lunch box, because his was green.

Yui came in after him. She saw Ken standing by the bench. "What are you looking at?" she asked. Ken pointed at the lunch box. "Someone left it here," he said. "Maybe there is lunch in it." Yui looked around, but no student was near the bench.

Ken opened the lunch box slowly. There was no rice in it. There was no egg in it. There was a small towel, a white ball, and a note. Yui took the note and read it aloud.

"Please help my friend. She is cold. I will come back."

The white ball moved under the towel.

Ken shut the lunch box at once. "We need a teacher," he said. Yui put her hand on the cover. "Wait," she said. "It may need air." Ken opened the box again. The white ball came out of the towel. It was not a ball. It was a very small bird.

The bird did not fly. It only looked at Ken and Yui. Ken put the towel around the bird. Yui kept the lunch box open. The bird got quiet. Ken said, "Who put a bird in a lunch box?" Yui looked at the note again. "Someone who wanted to help it," she said.

Then a girl ran to the shoe boxes. She had a small cup of water in one hand. She saw the open lunch box and stopped. "That is my lunch box," she said. "And that is my bird."

Her name was Hana. She found the bird on the ground near the school gate. The bird was cold, and Hana did not know what to do. She put it in her lunch box with her towel. Then she went to get water. She came back as fast as she could.

Ken gave the bird to Hana. Yui gave her the note. Hana smiled and said, "Thank you for not leaving her alone."

At that time, Mr. Sato came to the shoe boxes. He looked at the bird and then at the three students. "The bird needs a warm place," he said. "Let's take her to the teachers' room."

Hana held the bird carefully. Ken carried the blue lunch box. Yui carried the small cup.

The lunch box was empty again, but Hana was smiling.

After school, Hana came to Ken and Yui.

"The bird can fly now," she said.

Ken looked at the blue lunch box in Hana's hand.

"So it was a lunch box again?" he asked.

Hana opened it.

Inside were three small cookies.

"Today," she said, "it is a thank-you box."
