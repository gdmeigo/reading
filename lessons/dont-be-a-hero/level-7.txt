Don't Be a Hero

On Saturday, the town had a river clean-up. Daichi wanted to do something big. He brought a long net, old gloves, and a face that said, "I can do everything."

His grandmother came with him. She was seventy, but she walked faster than Daichi. "Don't be a hero," she said.

"I'm not," Daichi said. Then he saw a plastic bottle near the water. It was under a low bridge. Daichi ran to it.

"Don't be a hero," Grandma said again. "The stones are wet."

Daichi stopped. The bottle was close, but the river was fast. He felt a little angry. He wanted everyone to see him help.

Then he saw a small boy on the bank. The boy had a bag, but he did not pick anything up. He only looked at the water.

"What's wrong?" Daichi asked.

"My toy boat went under the bridge," the boy said.

"Then we should help him," Daichi said.

Daichi looked at Grandma. She gave him a short rope and a smile. "Let's think."

They tied the rope to Daichi's net. Daichi did not go into the water. He lay on the dry part of the bank, and Grandma held his belt. The boy held the end of the rope.

"Now," Grandma said.

The net went under the bridge. It came back with the bottle, two leaves, and a tiny blue boat.

The boy shouted, "My boat!"

Daichi laughed. He did not look like a hero. His shirt was dirty, and grass was in his hair. But the boy gave him the biggest smile of the day.

The other helpers clapped from the path.

Grandma patted his shoulder. "See? Don't be a hero. Be useful."

Daichi put the bottle in the trash bag. "That's harder," he said.

"Yes," Grandma said. "And better."
