The Red Pin Parade

Ken is in the room. Miki is in the room, too. A red pin is on Ken's shirt.

"You are number one," Miki says.

Ken looks at the pin. "I am number one?"

"Yes," Miki says. "You are first."

Ken walks to the door. Taro is there. A red pin is on Taro's shirt, too.

"He is number one," Ken says.

Taro looks at the pin. "I am number one?"

Now Emi comes in. A red pin is on her bag.

"She is number one," Taro says.

Emi smiles. "No. It is not a number. It is a sign."

The three students look at the red pins. One pin is on a shirt. One pin is on a shirt. One pin is on a bag. Then Ken sees a small paper near the desk.

The paper says, "Red pin people, come here."

"Here?" Ken asks.

Miki points at the window. Outside, Mr. Sato has a red pin on his hat. He is not number one. He is not number two. He is just very happy.

"Come here," he says.

The students go outside. Ten students are there. All of them have red pins.

Mr. Sato gives Ken a small flag. "It is a parade," he says. "It is for the new library."

Ken looks at the little parade. It is small. It is quiet. But it is funny.

Taro says, "I am number one in the quiet parade."

Emi says, "No. The library is number one."

Ken holds the flag. The red pin is not a mystery now. It is a tiny sign for a big smile.
