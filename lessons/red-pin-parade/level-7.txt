The Red Pin Parade

Kaito is in the room. Miki is in the room, too. A red pin is on Kaito's shirt.

"You are number one," Miki says.

Kaito looks at the pin. "I am number one?"

"Yes," Miki says. "You are first."

Kaito walks to the door. Ren is there. A red pin is on Ren's shirt, too.

"He is number one," Kaito says.

Ren looks at the pin. "I am number one?"

Now Miki comes in. A red pin is on her bag.

"She is number one," Ren says.

Miki smiles. "No. It is not a number. It is a sign."

The three students look at the red pins. One pin is on a shirt. One pin is on a shirt. One pin is on a bag. Then Kaito sees a small paper near the desk.

The paper says, "Red pin people, come here."

"Here?" Kaito asks.

Miki points at the window. Outside, Mr. Sato has a red pin on his hat. He is not number one. He is not number two. He is just very happy.

"Come here," he says.

The students go outside. Ten students are there. All of them have red pins.

Mr. Sato gives Kaito a small flag. "It is a parade," he says. "It is for the new library."

Kaito looks at the little parade. It is small. It is quiet. But it is funny.

Ren says, "I am number one in the quiet parade."

Miki says, "No. The library is number one."

Kaito holds the flag. The red pin is not a mystery now. It is a tiny sign for a big smile.
