Why the Lights Blink

"Why do the lights blink?" Kaito asks.

The science room is dark. The lights blink three times. Then they stop. Then they blink three times again.

Saki writes it down. "Three, stop, three."

Ren looks out the window. The town lights are normal. Only the school lights are strange.

"Maybe it is a machine problem," he says.

Kaito sees a small box under the teacher's desk. It has a green light. The green light blinks in the same way.

"Why is this box here?" Saki asks.

On the box is a note: "Do not open. Listen first."

Ren puts his ear near the box. "I hear a sound."

The sound is very soft. Beep, beep, beep. Stop. Beep, beep, beep.

Saki says, "It is like a message."

Kaito remembers a poster in the hall. It says, "Space Day: Send a light message."

"Maybe the astronomy club made it," he says.

They find the club on the roof. Miki, the club leader, is looking at the sky.

"Why are the school lights blinking?" Kaito asks.

Miki points at a small camera. "We are trying to send a light message to the old camera on the hill. But the signal is too big. It is using the hall lights, too."

"What does the message say?" Saki asks.

Miki smiles. "It says SOS."

Ren jumps. "SOS?"

"Snack Out Soon," Miki says. "The teacher put cookies in the science room."

Everyone laughs.

Kaito looks at the blinking lights again. They are not scary now. They are a strange invitation.

The lights blink three more times.

Saki says, "I know why."

Ren says, "Because cookies are calling."
