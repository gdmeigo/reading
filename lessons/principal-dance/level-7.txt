Can the Principal Dance?

The school festival was near, and everyone had a job. Ken made signs. Miki wrote names on the board. Suneta carried boxes, but he carried only the empty ones.

Jaio came into the room with a silver whistle.

"I am the dance leader," he said.

"Who said that?" Miki asked.

"My whistle said it," Jaio said. He blew it once. No sound came out.

Ken looked at the list. "The dance leader is Mr. Sato."

Jaio smiled. "Can Mr. Sato dance?"

No one answered. Mr. Sato was the principal. He walked slowly. He spoke slowly. Even his morning greeting looked slow.

Suneta ran to the door. "Mr. Sato is coming!"

Everyone stood in a line. Jaio hid the whistle in his pocket.

Mr. Sato came in with a small radio. "Good. Practice time."

Ken raised his hand. "Can you dance, Mr. Sato?"

Mr. Sato looked at Ken. The room was very quiet.

"Can I dance?" he said. "That is a good question."

He put the radio on the desk. The music started. It was fast.

Mr. Sato moved one foot. Then he moved the other foot. Then he turned. His glasses stayed still, but his body did not.

The class shouted.

Jaio opened his mouth. Suneta dropped an empty box.

Mr. Sato stopped in front of Jaio. "You have a whistle. Can you dance?"

Jaio looked at the floor. "Maybe."

"Good," said Mr. Sato. "Then you can dance in front."

Jaio wanted to say no, but everyone was looking at him. He took one step. Suneta took the same step behind him. Jaio took another step. Suneta took another step.

Soon the two boys were dancing like two broken machines.

Miki laughed so hard that she had to sit down.

At the festival, Mr. Sato danced in the back. Jaio and Suneta danced in the front. They were not good, but they were very serious.

Ken wrote a new sign: "Can the principal dance? Yes. Can Jaio dance? Please watch and decide."
