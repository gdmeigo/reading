The Star Bus

Ken and Mina got up early because they wanted to watch the star shower.
They waited at the old bus stop before sunrise.
The sign said LAST BUS: 5:40, but no one in town used that stop anymore.

At 5:39, a silver bus came through the fog.
It made no sound.
The driver opened the door and said, "Are you going to the sky?"

Mina looked at Ken.
"We are going to school," she said.

"Good," said the driver. "The sky is on the way."

Inside the bus, children were sitting quietly.
One boy was measuring a green light with a ruler.
One girl was writing quickly in a blue notebook.

Then Ken found a black bag on the last seat.
It was warm, and it moved like something was breathing inside.

"Do not open it," said the driver.

Of course, everyone looked at the bag.

The bus went up.
The town became small.
When the bag opened, a tiny star came out.
It was not bright.
It showed them a picture of the old music room at school.

Ken understood.
"It wants us to bring back the light," he said.
