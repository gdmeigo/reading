The Star Bus

Sora got up early because he wanted to watch the last star shower of the year. He put a small notebook, a pen, and a bottle of water in his bag. His sister Mina was not sure about the plan. "A star shower is nice," she said, "but why do we have to wait at the old bus stop?"

The bus stop was full of white dust. There was no bus there. There was only a sign which said, LAST BUS: 5:40. Sora looked at his watch. It was 5:39. Maybe nothing would happen.

Then the road became clear and bright. A silver bus came through the morning fog. It made no sound. The driver was an old woman with blue gloves. "Are you against the trip?" she asked.

"We do not know the trip," said Mina.

"Good," said the driver. "Then you can join it."

Sora and Mina got on the bus. Inside, there were children from many towns. Some were sleeping. Some were eating bread. One boy was trying to measure a small green light with a ruler. The light kept changing its shape.

The bus went up, but the wheels stayed on the road. Sora looked out of the window. The school, the river, and the station became small. Then they were not under the sky. They were in it.

At the last seat, Sora found a black bag. On it was one word: HOME. The bag was warm. It moved once.

"Do not open it," said the driver.

Of course, everyone looked at Sora.

Mina picked up the bag carefully. "Whose bag is this?"

No one answered.

Then the bag opened by itself. A tiny star came out. It was not hard or soft. It was like breath, light, and music together. It went to the window and pushed against the glass.

"It wants to go back," said Sora.

"Maybe," said Mina. "But maybe it wants us to bring something back."

The star showed them a picture in the window: their town, dark and quiet, with no lights in the old music room. Sora understood. The star shower was not falling. It was looking for places that had stopped shining.

The driver gave Sora a key. "Set up one light. That is enough."

The bus came back at 5:40. No one at school believed Sora and Mina, but that night they put one small lamp in the old music room. It was different from the other lights. It did not shine down. It shone up.

At midnight, the sky was full of stars. One small star moved quickly, as if it was waving.

Mina smiled. "How nice."

Sora wrote one sentence in his notebook: Some buses do not take people away. They bring the sky back.
