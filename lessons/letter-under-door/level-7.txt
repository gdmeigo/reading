The Letter Under the Door

Ken writes a letter to the music club.

"Please come to the hall at four," he writes. "We need your song."

He puts the letter on the music room desk. Then he goes back to class.

At lunch, the letter is under the hall door.

"Why is it here?" Ken asks.

Mika reads it. "It says the music club must come at four."

"I wrote it," Ken says. "But I put it in the music room."

Taro looks at the door. "Maybe the letter walked."

"Letters don't walk," Mika says.

They go to the music room. On the desk is another letter. It says, "Please come to the library at four."

Ken did not write that.

Now they are worried. The school event needs the music club and the library team. If everyone goes to different places, the event will start with no song and no books.

Mika finds a small pencil mark on the second letter. It is a star.

"Yui always writes a star," she says.

They find Yui near the stairs. She is holding many letters.

"I wanted to help," Yui says. "I wrote letters for all clubs. But I did not know which room was right."

Ken is not angry. "You helped a lot. Now we only need to write one more letter."

Together they write a clear letter:

"Please come to the hall at four. Music club, library team, and helpers."

Yui draws one small star at the bottom.

At four, everyone comes to the hall.

Taro whispers, "The letter did not walk."

Mika smiles. "No. A good helper ran."
