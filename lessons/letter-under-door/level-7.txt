The Letter Under the Door

Kaito writes a letter to the music club.

"Please come to the hall at four," he writes. "We need your song."

He puts the letter on the music room desk. Then he goes back to class.

At lunch, the letter is under the hall door.

"Why is it here?" Kaito asks.

Saki reads it. "It says the music club must come at four."

"I wrote it," Kaito says. "But I put it in the music room."

Ren looks at the door. "Maybe the letter walked."

"Letters don't walk," Saki says.

They go to the music room. On the desk is another letter. It says, "Please come to the library at four."

Kaito did not write that.

Now they are worried. The school event needs the music club and the library team. If everyone goes to different places, the event will start with no song and no books.

Saki finds a small pencil mark on the second letter. It is a star.

"Nana always writes a star," she says.

They find Nana near the stairs. She is holding many letters.

"I wanted to help," Nana says. "I wrote letters for all clubs. But I did not know which room was right."

Kaito is not angry. "You helped a lot. Now we only need to write one more letter."

Together they write a clear letter:

"Please come to the hall at four. Music club, library team, and helpers."

Nana draws one small star at the bottom.

At four, everyone comes to the hall.

Ren whispers, "The letter did not walk."

Saki smiles. "No. A good helper ran."
