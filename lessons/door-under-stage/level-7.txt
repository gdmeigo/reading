The Door Under the Stage

Ken can see a little door under the stage.

"Do you see it?" he asks.

Mika looks under the stage. "I see a box. I see a seat. I don't see a door."

Taro comes with a light. "I see it," he says. "It is small."

The drama club is cleaning the hall. The show is over. Everyone is tired, but Ken cannot stop looking at the little door.

"Maybe it is for old lights," Mika says.

"Maybe it is for a secret room," Taro says.

Ken opens the door. Inside is not a room. Inside is a wooden bird, a paper moon, and a blue cloth.

Mika picks up the wooden bird. "This is from the old school play."

There is also a notebook. Ken opens it. The first page says, "Things we must not lose."

Taro reads the list. "Bird. Moon. Sea cloth. Bell. Smile."

"Smile?" Mika says.

Ken turns the page. There is a photo. In the photo, young students stand on the stage. One student has the wooden bird. One student has the moon. A teacher holds the blue cloth.

At the bottom, someone wrote, "See you again."

The hall door opens. Mr. Sato comes in. He sees the notebook and gets very quiet.

"That is my class," he says.

"You were in the play?" Ken asks.

Mr. Sato points at a small boy in the photo. "That is me. I lost the wooden bird after the show. I was sad for a week."

Mika gives him the bird.

Mr. Sato smiles. "Now the list is right."

Taro looks at the last word. "Smile."

Ken says, "We found that, too."
