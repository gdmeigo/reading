The Last Bus

Five graded text-only readings.

Level 1

Assumed grading: present be/have/look/come/see/give/say, bus stop, simple nouns.

Yui is at the bus stop.

Ken is with Yui.

An old woman is there.

She has a small bag.

She looks sad.

The bus comes.

The woman does not get on.

Ken sees a card on the bench.

It says, "Hospital."

Yui gives the card to the woman.

The woman smiles.

"Thank you," she says.

Level 2

Assumed grading: Level 1 plus came/was/sitting/did not/helped, window.

Ken and Yui came to the bus stop after school.

An old woman was sitting on the bench.

She had a small bag.

The bus came.

The woman looked at the bus.

But she did not get on.

Ken saw a card on the bench.

It said, "Hospital."

Yui gave the card to the woman.

"Is this yours?" she asked.

The woman looked at the card.

"Yes," she said.

"I need this bus."

Ken and Yui helped her get on.

The woman smiled from the window.

Level 3

Assumed grading: Level 2 plus getting dark, last bus, wrong/gone, husband, next day.

Ken and Yui came to the bus stop after school.

The sky was getting dark.

An old woman was sitting on the bench.

She had a small bag and a white card.

The last bus came.

The woman stood up.

Then she sat down again.

"Is something wrong?" asked Yui.

The woman looked in her bag.

"My card is gone," she said.

Ken looked under the bench.

There was a white card.

It said, "City Hospital."

Yui gave it to the woman.

The woman looked very happy.

"My husband is in the hospital," she said.

"I need this bus."

Ken helped her with the bag.

Yui told the driver to wait.

The woman got on the bus.

Before the bus went away, she waved from the window.

The next day, Ken and Yui saw the woman again.

This time, she was with her husband.

They both said, "Thank you."

Level 4

Assumed grading: Level 3 plus every, quickly, worried, one more minute, same bus stop.

Ken and Yui came to the bus stop after school.

It was getting dark, and the street lights were on.

An old woman was sitting on the bench with a small bag.

She looked at every bus, but she did not get on.

"Maybe she is waiting for someone," said Ken.

Then the last bus came.

The woman stood up quickly.

She opened her bag and looked inside.

Her face changed.

"Is something wrong?" asked Yui.

"My hospital card is gone," said the woman.

"My husband is waiting for me."

Ken looked under the bench.

Yui looked near the sign.

The bus driver waited, but he looked at the clock.

Then Ken saw a white card under a fallen leaf.

It said, "City Hospital."

Yui gave the card to the woman.

The woman held it with both hands.

"Thank you. I was so worried."

Ken carried her small bag.

Yui asked the driver, "Can you wait one more minute?"

The driver smiled.

"For this, yes."

The woman got on the bus.

She waved from the window.

The next day, Ken and Yui saw her again at the same bus stop.

This time, an old man was with her.

"This is my husband," she said.

The man bowed.

"Thank you for helping my wife."

Then he gave them two warm buns from a paper bag.

Level 5

Assumed grading: Level 4 plus longer scene, already, corner, slowly, warm final line.

Ken and Yui came to the bus stop after school. It was getting dark, and the street lights were already on. An old woman was sitting on the bench with a small bag on her knees. She looked at every bus that came, but she did not get on.

"Maybe she is waiting for someone," said Ken.

Then the last bus for City Hospital came around the corner. The woman stood up quickly. She opened her bag, looked inside, and then stopped. Her face changed.

Yui went to her. "Is something wrong?" she asked.

"My hospital card is gone," said the woman. "My husband is waiting for me. I have to take this bus."

The driver opened the door, but he looked at the clock. Ken looked under the bench. Yui looked near the bus stop sign. The woman looked in her bag again and again.

Then Ken saw something white under a fallen leaf. It was a card. On the card were the words "City Hospital."

"Is this it?" asked Ken.

The woman held the card with both hands. "Yes. Thank you. I was so worried."

Ken carried her small bag. Yui asked the driver, "Can you wait one more minute?"

The driver looked at the old woman, then at Ken and Yui. "For this, yes," he said.

The woman got on the bus and sat by the window. Before the bus left, she waved to Ken and Yui.

The next day, Ken and Yui saw her again at the same bus stop. This time, an old man was with her.

"This is my husband," she said. "He came home from the hospital today."

The man bowed slowly. "Thank you for helping my wife."

Then he opened a paper bag. Inside were two warm buns.

"We bought these on the way home," he said. "Please take them."

Ken and Yui took the buns.

The last bus had gone, but the bus stop felt warm.
