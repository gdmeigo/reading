The Last Bus

Ken and Yui came to the bus stop after school. It was getting
dark, and the street lights were already on. An old woman was
sitting on the bench with a small bag on her knees. She looked at
every bus that came, but she did not get on.

"Maybe she is waiting for someone," said Ken.

Then the last bus for City Hospital came around the corner. The
woman stood up quickly. She opened her bag, looked inside, and
then stopped. Her face changed.

Yui went to her. "Is something wrong?" she asked.

"My hospital card is gone," said the woman. "My husband is
waiting for me. I have to take this bus."

The driver opened the door, but he looked at the clock. Ken
looked under the bench. Yui looked near the bus stop sign. The
woman looked in her bag again and again.

Then Ken saw something white under a fallen leaf. It was a card.
On the card were the words "City Hospital."

"Is this it?" asked Ken.

The woman held the card with both hands. "Yes. Thank you. I
was so worried."

Ken carried her small bag. Yui asked the driver, "Can you wait
one more minute?"

The driver looked at the old woman, then at Ken and Yui.
"For this, yes," he said.

The woman got on the bus and sat by the window. Before the bus
left, she waved to Ken and Yui.

The next day, Ken and Yui saw her again at the same bus stop.
This time, an old man was with her.

"This is my husband," she said. "He came home from the
hospital today."

The man bowed slowly. "Thank you for helping my wife."

Then he opened a paper bag. Inside were two warm buns.

"We bought these on the way home," he said. "Please take them."

Ken and Yui took the buns.
The last bus had gone, but the bus stop felt warm.
