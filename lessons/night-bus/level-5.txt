The Last Bus

Ren and Mika came to the bus stop after school.
It was getting dark, and the street lights were on.
An old woman was sitting on the bench with a small bag.
She looked at every bus, but she did not get on.

"Maybe she is waiting for someone," said Ren.

Then the last bus came.
The woman stood up quickly.
She opened her bag and looked inside.
Her face changed.

"Is something wrong?" asked Mika.
"My hospital card is gone," said the woman.
"My husband is waiting for me."

Ren looked under the bench.
Mika looked near the sign.
The bus driver waited, but he looked at the clock.

Then Ren saw a white card under a fallen leaf.
It said, "City Hospital."

Mika gave the card to the woman.
The woman held it with both hands.
"Thank you. I was so worried."

Ren carried her small bag.
Mika asked the driver, "Can you wait one more minute?"

The driver smiled.
"For this, yes."

The woman got on the bus.
She waved from the window.

The next day, Ren and Mika saw her again at the same bus stop.
This time, an old man was with her.
"This is my husband," she said.

The man bowed.
"Thank you for helping my wife."

Then he gave them two warm buns from a paper bag.
