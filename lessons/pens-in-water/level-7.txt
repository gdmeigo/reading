The Pens in the Water

These pens are on the desk. They are Ken's pens. One pen is blue. One pen is red. One pen is green.

Mika puts a glass of water near them.

"Don't put water there," Ken says.

"It is for a trick," Mika says.

Taro comes in with a paper. On the paper is a long red line. "I need the red pen," he says.

Ken gives it to him. Taro puts the pen in the glass for one second.

"Stop!" Ken says. "That is my pen."

Taro points at the glass. "Look."

The red pen looks short in the water. The top of the pen is in one place. The bottom of the pen looks in another place.

Mika says, "It is not broken. Water changes the way we see it."

Ken looks again. He takes the pen out. The pen is long again.

Then Emi runs in. "Where are the green and blue pens?"

Ken looks at the desk. The pens are not there.

Mika looks at the glass. "Maybe the trick is not the only trick."

Taro points under the paper. The green pen is there. Emi laughs. "Sorry. I put it there."

"But where is the blue pen?" Ken asks.

The room gets quiet. Then Ken sees a blue line on the white card in the glass. The card is behind the water, so the line looks like a small door.

Ken lifts the card. The blue pen is behind it.

Mika smiles. "Water can hide a pen. Paper can hide a pen, too."

Ken takes all three pens. "These pens are not for tricks now."

Taro says, "Then what are they for?"

Ken writes on the paper: "Science club at lunch."
