The Pens in the Water

These pens are on the desk. They are Kaito's pens. One pen is blue. One pen is red. One pen is green.

Saki puts a glass of water near them.

"Don't put water there," Kaito says.

"It is for a trick," Saki says.

Ren comes in with a paper. On the paper is a long red line. "I need the red pen," he says.

Kaito gives it to him. Ren puts the pen in the glass for one second.

"Stop!" Kaito says. "That is my pen."

Ren points at the glass. "Look."

The red pen looks short in the water. The top of the pen is in one place. The bottom of the pen looks in another place.

Saki says, "It is not broken. Water changes the way we see it."

Kaito looks again. He takes the pen out. The pen is long again.

Then Miki runs in. "Where are the green and blue pens?"

Kaito looks at the desk. The pens are not there.

Saki looks at the glass. "Maybe the trick is not the only trick."

Ren points under the paper. The green pen is there. Miki laughs. "Sorry. I put it there."

"But where is the blue pen?" Kaito asks.

The room gets quiet. Then Kaito sees a blue line on the white card in the glass. The card is behind the water, so the line looks like a small door.

Kaito lifts the card. The blue pen is behind it.

Saki smiles. "Water can hide a pen. Paper can hide a pen, too."

Kaito takes all three pens. "These pens are not for tricks now."

Ren says, "Then what are they for?"

Kaito writes on the paper: "Science club at lunch."
