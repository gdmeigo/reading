Show Me the Seat

The class has a special guest today. Mr. Ito is old, but he walks very fast.

"Please show Mr. Ito his seat," Ms. Tanaka says to Ken.

Ken nods. "This way, please."

But the classroom is different today. The desks are in a circle for a talk event. There are name cards on the desks.

Ken looks for "Ito." He finds "Mito." He finds "Sato." He finds "Taro," but Taro is not a guest.

Mr. Ito smiles. "Can you show me the seat?"

"Yes," Ken says. "Maybe."

Mika comes over. "I can teach you the secret," she says. "The guests have blue cards."

Ken finds a blue card. It says "Ito." But there is a bag on the chair.

Taro runs in. "That is my bag."

"Why is your bag on Mr. Ito's seat?" Mika asks.

Taro looks embarrassed. "I wanted to save a good seat for the guest."

Mr. Ito laughs. "Then you did a good job. You gave my seat a bag."

Taro moves the bag. Ken shows Mr. Ito the seat.

The talk begins. Mr. Ito teaches the class an old paper game. He gives each student a square paper. He shows them how to fold it.

Ken cannot make the last fold. Taro cannot, either. Mika can.

"Please teach me," Ken says.

Mika shows Ken the fold. Ken shows Taro. Taro shows Mr. Ito a very strange paper bird.

"It is a new kind of bird," Mr. Ito says.

At the end, Ken writes a note on the board: "Show someone a seat. Teach someone a fold. Give someone a laugh."

Mr. Ito points at the note. "That is the best lesson today."
