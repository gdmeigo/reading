Show Me the Seat

The class has a special guest today. Mr. Ito is old, but he walks very fast.

"Please show Mr. Ito his seat," Ms. Tanaka says to Kaito.

Kaito nods. "This way, please."

But the classroom is different today. The desks are in a circle for a talk event. There are name cards on the desks.

Kaito looks for "Ito." He finds "Mito." He finds "Sato." He finds "Ren," but Ren is not a guest.

Mr. Ito smiles. "Can you show me the seat?"

"Yes," Kaito says. "Maybe."

Saki comes over. "I can teach you the secret," she says. "The guests have blue cards."

Kaito finds a blue card. It says "Ito." But there is a bag on the seat.

Ren runs in. "That is my bag."

"Why is your bag on Mr. Ito's seat?" Saki asks.

Ren looks embarrassed. "I wanted to save a good seat for the guest."

Mr. Ito laughs. "Then you did a good job. You gave my seat a bag."

Ren moves the bag. Kaito shows Mr. Ito the seat.

The talk begins. Mr. Ito teaches the class an old paper game. He gives each student a square paper. He shows them how to fold it.

Kaito cannot make the last fold. Ren cannot, either. Saki can.

"Please teach me," Kaito says.

Saki shows Kaito the fold. Kaito shows Ren. Ren shows Mr. Ito a very strange paper bird.

"It is a new kind of bird," Mr. Ito says.

At the end, Kaito writes a note on the board: "Show someone a seat. Teach someone a fold. Give someone a laugh."

Mr. Ito points at the note. "That is the best lesson today."
