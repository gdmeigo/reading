The Map Under the Stage

"Where is the map?" Hana asks.

The class is in the hall. The school show starts at three. Hana has a map for the stage. Ken has the lights. Taro has the music.

"It is on the piano," Ken says.

But the piano is empty.

"Where is it?" Hana asks again.

Taro looks under the seat. Ken looks behind the curtain. Hana looks in her bag. There is no map.

Then a small voice comes from the stage. "Where is the bird?"

Everyone stops.

"Bird?" Ken says.

Yui, a first-year student, comes out from behind a box. She has a paper bird in her hand.

"The map has a bird on it," she says. "I saw it."

Hana remembers. There is a small bird mark on the map. The bird shows the place for the first box.

Ken asks, "Where did you see it?"

Yui points down. "There."

Under the stage is a narrow space. Ken cannot go in. Taro cannot go in. Yui can.

"Don't go alone," Hana says. "I can see you from here."

Yui goes under the stage. She finds a paper, but it is not the map. It is a menu from the lunch room.

Then she sees a red bird mark on the back of a board.

"Here!" she says.

Ken pulls the board out. The map is taped to the back.

Taro looks at the board. "Who taped it here?"

Hana sees her own note on the map: "Do not lose this."

She laughs. "I taped it here yesterday. I wanted a safe place."

Ken smiles. "Where is the safest place?"

Hana says, "Not under the stage."
