The Map Under the Stage

"Where is the map?" Nana asks.

The class is in the hall. The school show starts at three. Nana has a map for the stage. Kaito has the lights. Ren has the music.

"It is on the piano," Kaito says.

But the piano is empty.

"Where is it?" Nana asks again.

Ren looks under the seat. Kaito looks behind the curtain. Nana looks in her bag. There is no map.

Then a small voice comes from the stage. "Where is the bird?"

Everyone stops.

"Bird?" Kaito says.

Nana, a first-year student, comes out from behind a box. She has a paper bird in her hand.

"The map has a bird on it," she says. "I saw it."

Nana remembers. There is a small bird mark on the map. The bird shows the place for the first box.

Kaito asks, "Where did you see it?"

Nana points down. "There."

Under the stage is a narrow space. Kaito cannot go in. Ren cannot go in. Nana can.

"Don't go alone," Nana says. "I can see you from here."

Nana goes under the stage. She finds a paper, but it is not the map. It is a menu from the lunch room.

Then she sees a red bird mark on the back of a board.

"Here!" she says.

Kaito pulls the board out. The map is taped to the back.

Ren looks at the board. "Who taped it here?"

Nana sees her own note on the map: "Do not lose this."

She laughs. "I taped it here yesterday. I wanted a safe place."

Kaito smiles. "Where is the safest place?"

Nana says, "Not under the stage."
