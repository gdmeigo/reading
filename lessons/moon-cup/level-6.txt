The Moon Cup

Yui visited Grandma's house during the summer festival. The house was old, but every room was full of small bright things: glass bells, red paper birds, and cups which looked too thin to use.

"Do not drink from the blue cup after sunset," said Grandma.

Of course, that was the sentence that made Yui look at the cup again. It was on a shelf between two white cups. When she looked through it, the room was upside down.

After dinner, Ken came with festival tickets. Yui put a little water in the cup. The water did not go down. It rose like steam, but it was cold. Then a small moon appeared above the cup.

"I am not sure about this house," said the moon. "I lost my shadow."

Without the shadow, the festival lanterns would not shine. Yui and Ken looked through the house. A box? A bag? A bottle? Then Ken saw a festival drum. It was round like a full moon, but it was empty inside. On the back of it was a black circle.

Grandma came in and smiled. She hit the drum once.

The black circle jumped into the blue cup. Outside, every lantern in the street turned on.

"Why did you say not to drink?" asked Yui.

"Because people who drink moon water talk in poems for three days," said Grandma.

Ken looked at her. "Did you drink it before?"

Grandma only smiled.
