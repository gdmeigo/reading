The Moon Cup

Yui visited Grandma's house during the summer festival.
The house was full of beautiful old things: glass bells, red paper birds, and cups which looked too thin to use.

On the highest shelf, there was a blue cup.

"Do not drink from that cup after sunset," said Grandma.

That rule made Yui want to know more.

After dinner, Ken came with festival tickets.
Yui showed him the cup. When she looked through it, the room was upside down, and the moon in the window moved the wrong way.

Yui put water in the cup.
The water rose like cold steam.
A small moon appeared above it.

"I have lost my shadow," said the moon. "Without it, the festival lanterns will not shine."

Ken and Yui looked through the house.
They found many round things, but only one looked full and had nothing inside: the festival drum.
On the back of it was a black circle.

Grandma came in and hit the drum once.
The black circle jumped into the blue cup.

Outside, every lantern in the street turned on at the same time.

"Why did you say not to drink?" asked Ken.

Grandma smiled. "Moon water makes people talk in poems."
