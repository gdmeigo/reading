The Little Door

Five graded text-only readings.

Level 1

Ken has a small key.

Yui sees the key.

The key is not new.

It is old.

Ken puts the key on the desk.

Yui sees a small door under the desk.

The door is shut.

Ken puts the key in the door.

The door opens.

Ken and Yui see a red bag.

The bag is small.

Something is in the bag.

The bag moves.

Ken looks at Yui.

Yui looks at Ken.

"What is in the bag?"

Level 2

Ken came to school with a small key.

He put the key on his desk.

Yui came to Ken's desk.

She saw the key.

"What is that?" she said.

"It is a key," said Ken.

"But it is not my key."

Then Yui saw a small door under Ken's desk.

The door was shut.

Ken put the key in the door.

The door opened.

Ken and Yui saw a red bag.

The bag was in the small door.

Ken took the bag out.

The bag moved.

Ken did not open it.

Yui did not open it.

Then the bag moved again.

Ken said, "I think something is in this bag."

Level 3

Ken came into the classroom before class.

He had an old key in his hand.

He did not know the key.

He found it in his shoe box.

Yui came to his desk.

"What do you have?" she asked.

"An old key," said Ken.

"It was in my shoe box."

Yui looked under Ken's desk.

There was a very small door.

It was behind a book.

Ken and Yui did not see the door yesterday.

Ken put the key in the door.

The door opened.

In the door they saw a red bag.

Ken took it out and put it on the desk.

The bag moved.

Ken and Yui moved back.

"Open it," said Yui.

"No," said Ken. "You open it."

The bag moved again.

Then they heard a small sound.

It was not a bird.

It was not a cat.

It was under the red bag.

Ken took the bag off the desk.

There was another key.

This key was gold.

Level 4

Ken came to school early.

He had an old key in his pocket.

He found it in his shoe box in the morning.

There was no name on the key.

There was only one small star.

Yui came into the classroom and saw the key.

"Is that your house key?" she asked.

"No," said Ken. "I don't know this key."

Yui looked at the star.

Then she looked at Ken's desk.

There was a small star under the desk, too.

It was on a small door.

The door was behind a book and a box.

Ken put the key in the small door.

The door opened.

Ken and Yui looked in.

They saw a red bag.

Ken took the bag out and put it on the desk.

The bag moved.

Yui took one step back.

Ken took two steps back.

"Something is in it," said Yui.

Then the bag stopped.

Ken opened it very slowly.

There was no animal in the bag.

There was another key.

This key was gold.

There was a word on it: MUSIC.

At that time, the school bell rang.

The music room was shut every morning.

But today, Ken had a gold key in his hand.

"Do we go?" asked Yui.

Ken looked at the key.

Then he looked at the door.

"Yes," he said. "But not alone."

Level 5

Ken came to school at 7:40 a.m. He was the first student in the classroom. He had an old key in his pocket. He found it in his shoe box that morning. There was no name on it. There was only one small star on the top of the key.

Yui came into the classroom with her bag. She saw Ken at his desk. "What do you have, Ken?" she asked. Ken put the key on the desk. "I don't know," he said. "It was in my shoe box."

Yui looked at the key. Then she looked under Ken's desk. There was a small star there, too. It was not on a paper. It was on a small door. The door was behind a book and a box. Ken and Yui moved the book and the box. The door was very small, but the old key went into it.

Ken turned the key. The door opened.

In the small door they saw a red bag. Ken took the bag out and put it on his desk. The bag moved. Yui took one step back. Ken took two steps back. "There is something in it," said Yui. Ken did not open the bag. He looked at the door. The door was shut again.

Then the bag moved again. It made a small sound. Ken opened the bag very slowly. There was no bird in it. There was no cat in it. There was another key. This key was gold. On the gold key there was one word: MUSIC.

At that time, Mr. Sato came into the classroom. He saw the key in Ken's hand. "Where did you get that?" he asked. Ken said, "It was in the red bag." Mr. Sato did not laugh. He went to the window and looked at the old music room.

"The music room has been shut for ten years," said Mr. Sato. "But this key was in my desk when I was a student here."

Ken and Yui looked at the gold key again.

Then they heard music.

It came from the shut music room.
