The Little Door

Ken came to school at 7:40 a.m. He was the first student in the
classroom. He had an old key in his pocket. He found it in his
shoe box that morning. There was no name on it. There was only
one small star on the top of the key.

Mika came into the classroom with her bag. She saw Ken at his
desk. "What do you have, Ken?" she asked. Ken put the key on
the desk. "I don't know," he said. "It was in my shoe box."

Mika looked at the key. Then she looked under Ken's desk. There
was a small star there, too. It was not on a paper. It was on a
small door. The door was behind a book and a box. Ken and Mika
moved the book and the box. The door was very small, but the
old key went into it.

Ken turned the key. The door opened.

In the small door they saw a red bag. Ken took the bag out and
put it on his desk. The bag moved. Mika took one step back. Ken
took two steps back. "There is something in it," said Mika. Ken
did not open the bag. He looked at the door. The door was shut
again.

Then the bag moved again. It made a small sound. Ken opened
the bag very slowly. There was no bird in it. There was no cat in
it. There was another key. This key was gold. On the gold key
there was one word: MUSIC.

At that time, Mr. Sato came into the classroom. He saw the key
in Ken's hand. "Where did you get that?" he asked. Ken said,
"It was in the red bag." Mr. Sato did not laugh. He went to the
window and looked at the old music room.

"The music room was shut ten years ago," said Mr. Sato.
"But this key was in my desk when I was a student here."

Ken and Mika looked at the gold key again.

Then they heard music.

It came from the shut music room.
