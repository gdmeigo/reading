The Little Door, Part 2

Ken thought the small door under his desk was finished with him.

For one week, nothing happened. The old music room stayed quiet. The gold key stayed in Mr. Sato's desk. Ken and Mika tried to talk about normal things: homework, club practice, lunch, the weather. But every time the classroom became quiet, Ken looked under his desk.

On Friday, there was a new mark on the little door.

It was not the star from before. It was a word, written in thin white letters: AGAIN.

Mika saw it before Ken could hide it. "Maybe the door wants something."

"Maybe it wants us to leave it alone," said Ken.

But the red bag appeared at lunch. It was under Mika's seat, although no one had seen it before. Inside was not a key this time. There was a small notebook, old and soft, with Mr. Sato's name on the first page.

They took it to him after school.

Mr. Sato did not look surprised. He looked sad.

"I wrote this when I was twelve," he said. "I had a friend named Riku. We found the door together. One day he moved away before I could give this back."

The notebook was full of short songs. The last page had an address.

Ken, Mika, and Mr. Sato went there on Sunday. Riku was an old man now, but when he saw the notebook, he laughed like a student.

"I thought the door forgot me," he said.

That night, music came from the old room again. This time it was not lonely. It sounded like two boys playing together after many years.
