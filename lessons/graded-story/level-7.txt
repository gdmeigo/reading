The Little Door, Part 2

The red bag moved in Ken's hands.

Ken and Mika looked at it for a long time. They did not open it. The small door under the desk was still open, and it was very dark inside.

"Can you hear that?" Mika asked.

Ken listened. A small sound came from the bag. It was not a voice. It was a little song, soft and slow.

"This is not our bag," Ken said. "We should show it to Mr. Sato."

After school, they took the bag to Mr. Sato. He was in the old music room. When he saw the red bag, his face changed.

"Where did you find this?" he asked.

"In a little door under Ken's desk," Mika said.

Mr. Sato sat down. "I saw that door when I was twelve."

Ken opened the bag carefully. Inside was a small notebook and a gold key. The notebook had Mr. Sato's name on the first page. On the last page, there was one name: Riku.

Mr. Sato looked at the name for a long time.

"Riku was my best friend," he said. "We made songs together in this room. One day he moved away. I wanted to give this notebook back, but I lost the key."

The gold key was warm in Ken's hand. He put it in an old box beside the piano. The box opened.

Inside was a letter from Riku.

Mr. Sato read it quietly. Then he smiled.

On Sunday, Ken, Mika, and Mr. Sato visited Riku. Riku was an old man now, but when he saw the notebook, he laughed like a student.

"I waited for this song," Riku said.

That night, Ken looked under his desk. The little door was gone. But from the old music room, he heard the same small song. This time it was not sad. It sounded like two friends meeting again.
