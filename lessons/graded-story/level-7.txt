The Little Door, Part 2

The red bag moved in Ken's hands.

Ken and Mika looked at it for a long time. They did not open it. The little door under the desk was still open, and it was very dark inside.

"Put the bag back," Mika said.

Ken put the red bag in front of the little door. The bag stopped moving. Then one white card came out of the bag.

On the card, Ken saw three words.

GIVE TO SATO.

"Mr. Sato?" Ken said.

"Maybe," Mika said. "But why is his name in your desk?"

After school, Ken and Mika took the red bag to Mr. Sato. He was their music teacher. He looked at the card, and his face changed.

"Where did you find this?" he asked.

"In a little door under Ken's desk," Mika said.

Mr. Sato sat down. "I saw that door when I was twelve."

Ken still had the small key from the morning. The key was warm now.

"May I try it?" Mr. Sato asked.

Ken gave him the key. Mr. Sato put it in a tiny lock on the red bag. The bag opened.

Inside was a small notebook. On the first page were two names: Sato and Riku.

Mr. Sato read the names quietly.

"Riku was my best friend," he said. "We wrote songs together. One day, he moved away. I thought he took this notebook. Maybe he thought I had it."

On the last page, Mika found an old address and one short line.

I want to finish our song.

On Sunday, Ken, Mika, and Mr. Sato went to the address. Riku was an old man now. When he saw the red bag and the notebook, he laughed like a student.

"The door gave it back," Riku said.

Mr. Sato smiled. "Then we can finish the song."

The next day, Ken looked under his desk. The little door was gone. But in music class, Mr. Sato played a new song with Riku. It was soft and happy.

Ken looked at Mika. The red bag did not move now. Its work was done.
