The Little Door

Ken came to school early.
He had an old key in his pocket.
He found it in his shoe box in the morning.
There was no name on the key.
There was only one small star.

Yui came into the classroom and saw the key.
"Is that your house key?" she asked.
"No," said Ken. "I don't know this key."

Yui looked at the star.
Then she looked at Ken's desk.
There was a small star under the desk, too.
It was on a small door.
The door was behind a book and a box.

Ken put the key in the small door.
The door opened.
Ken and Yui looked in.
They saw a red bag.
Ken took the bag out and put it on the desk.

The bag moved.
Yui took one step back.
Ken took two steps back.
"Something is in it," said Yui.

Then the bag stopped.
Ken opened it very slowly.
There was no animal in the bag.
There was another key.
This key was gold.
There was a word on it: MUSIC.

At that time, the school bell rang.
The music room was shut every morning.
But today, Ken had a gold key in his hand.

"Do we go?" asked Yui.
Ken looked at the key.
Then he looked at the door.

"Yes," he said. "But not alone."

They went to Mr. Sato.
Mr. Sato looked at the gold key.
Then he smiled.

"I know this key," he said.
"It is for the old music box."

They went to the music room together.
Ken put the gold key in a small box.
The box opened.

Music came out.
There was a card in the box.
It said, "For the next music club."

Yui looked at Ken.
"Maybe that is us," she said.

Ken smiled.
"Maybe it is."
