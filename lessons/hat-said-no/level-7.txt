Because the Hat Said No

It was clean-up day. The class had to move desks, books, boxes, and one very old hat.

The hat was on the teacher's desk. It was black. It was tall. It looked important, but no one knew why.

"I will take it," Taro said.

"No," said Jaio. "I will take it because I am strong."

He put the hat on his head. It came down over his eyes.

Hana laughed. "You cannot see."

"I can see everything," Jaio said. He walked into the wall.

Suneta ran to him. "The wall moved!"

"The wall did not move," Taro said.

Jaio took one step back. "Then the hat moved because the hat is strange."

At that moment, Ms. Kato came in. "Please be careful with that hat. It is for the play."

"What play?" Hana asked.

"The king's play," said Ms. Kato. "The king cannot speak until the hat is on his head."

Jaio stood very straight. "Then I am the king."

Ms. Kato looked at him. "Good. The king's first job is to carry these seats."

Jaio did not move.

"Why are you still there?" Hana asked.

Jaio pointed at the hat. "Because the hat said no."

Everyone looked at the hat.

Suneta nodded. "I heard it."

"You did not," said Taro.

Ms. Kato smiled. "Then the hat can stay here. The king can carry the seats."

Jaio took off the hat at once. "I am not the king now."

But it was too late. Hana put a paper crown on his head.

"The hat said no," she said. "But the crown says go."

So Jaio carried the seats. Suneta carried one small book and looked tired. Taro carried the hat. At the end, Ms. Kato gave everyone juice because the room was clean.

Jaio drank his juice and stood up.

"You are welcome," he said.

"For what?" Hana asked.

Jaio pointed at the juice. "A good king works, and his people drink."

Suneta held up his cup. "Long live the king."

Hana laughed. "Then the king should clean again tomorrow."

Jaio looked at the hat. "The king is suddenly very tired."
