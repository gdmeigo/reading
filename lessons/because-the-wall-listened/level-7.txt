Because the Wall Listened

The art room wall had a problem.
Every morning, one picture was on the floor.

Ken put the picture back.
The next morning, it was down again.

"Maybe someone does not like this picture," Taro said.

"No," Mika said. "Look at the tape. It is still strong."

The picture showed a green hill and a small house.
Under the house, someone had written one word: "Listen."

Ken put his ear to the wall.
At first he heard nothing.
Then he heard a soft sound.

Tap. Tap. Tap.

Mika counted the taps.
"Three, two, one."

The same sound came again.

They moved the picture and found a tiny hole in the wall.
Behind the hole was an old clock from the next room.
It was not working well.
Its small hammer hit the wall every hour.

"So the picture falls because the wall shakes," Ken said.

But Mika was not finished.
She looked through the hole and saw a paper behind the clock.

They asked Mr. Sato to open the next room.
Behind the clock was a note.
It said, "Please find my hill picture."

Mr. Sato read the name and smiled.
"This was written by my first art student. She made that green hill picture."

Ken put the picture on a different wall.
It did not fall.

Taro looked at the old wall.
"The wall was not angry."

Mika nodded.
"It was listening for someone."
