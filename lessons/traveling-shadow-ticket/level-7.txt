The Traveling Shadow Ticket

Ken found an old train ticket in the library.
It was black, but its shadow was blue.

"This ticket wants to travel," Mika said.

Riku laughed.
"Tickets do not want anything."

At lunch, the ticket was gone.
Only the blue shadow stayed on the desk.

Ken touched the shadow.
It felt like cold paper.

After school, they visited the town station.
The same blue shadow was on the ticket machine.
Ken listened carefully.
He heard a very small voice.

"Platform three," it said.

The station had only two platforms.
Mika looked behind a map.
There was a narrow door with an old number: 3.

They opened it and found a small waiting room.
Dust was on the seats, but one seat was clean.
On the clean seat was the black ticket.

Ken picked it up.
The blue shadow moved to the wall and became a picture of a girl.

The girl was smiling at an old train.

Mika read the date on the ticket.
"It is from the day this station opened."

An old station worker came in.
"My sister lost that ticket," he said quietly. "She wanted to ride the first train, but she was sick that day."

Ken gave him the ticket.

The blue shadow grew warm and disappeared.

Riku looked at the empty wall.
"Maybe the ticket traveled at last."

The worker smiled.
"Yes. It came home."
