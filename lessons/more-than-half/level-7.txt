More Than Half

The class is making a wall picture for the school festival. It is a big picture of the town.

Kaito paints the river. Saki paints the station. Ren paints a dog, but the dog looks like a potato.

"It is a dog," Ren says.

"It is more than a dog," Saki says. "It is a mystery."

They laugh, but then Kaito sees the clock. The picture is not finished. More than half of the paper is still white.

"We cannot finish," he says.

Saki looks around the room. Only five students are working. The other students are at club practice.

"We need more than five people," she says.

Kaito runs to the hall. He sees Nana and three first-year students.

"Can you help us?" he asks.

Nana looks worried. "We are not good at painting."

"You can paint small windows," Kaito says.

Soon more students come. Some paint trees. Some paint clouds. Kaito, Nana's brother, paints many tiny birds. One bird is bigger than the station.

Ren says, "That bird is more than a bird. It is a plane."

At four, more than half of the picture is full of color. At five, only one white space is left.

Saki says, "What goes here?"

Kaito thinks. Then he paints the classroom with everyone inside it.

On festival day, people stop in front of the picture.

"This town looks happy," one teacher says.

Ren points at the potato dog. "That is my dog."

Nana points at the small windows. "Those are ours."

Kaito smiles. More than half of the picture was white at first. Now more than half of the class is in it.
