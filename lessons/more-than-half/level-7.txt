More Than Half

The class is making a wall picture for the school festival. It is a big picture of the town.

Ken paints the river. Mika paints the station. Taro paints a dog, but the dog looks like a potato.

"It is a dog," Taro says.

"It is more than a dog," Mika says. "It is a mystery."

They laugh, but then Ken sees the clock. The picture is not finished. More than half of the paper is still white.

"We cannot finish," he says.

Mika looks around the room. Only five students are working. The other students are at club practice.

"We need more than five people," she says.

Ken runs to the hall. He sees Yui and three first-year students.

"Can you help us?" he asks.

Yui looks worried. "We are not good at painting."

"You can paint small windows," Ken says.

Soon more students come. Some paint trees. Some paint clouds. Sota, Rina's brother, paints many tiny birds. One bird is bigger than the station.

Taro says, "That bird is more than a bird. It is a plane."

At four, more than half of the picture is full of color. At five, only one white space is left.

Mika says, "What goes here?"

Ken thinks. Then he paints the classroom with everyone inside it.

On festival day, people stop in front of the picture.

"This town looks happy," one teacher says.

Taro points at the potato dog. "That is my dog."

Yui points at the small windows. "Those are ours."

Ken smiles. More than half of the picture was white at first. Now more than half of the class is in it.
