How to Read the Stone

There was a flat gray stone in the school garden.
No one knew why it was there.

One day, Yui washed it with water.
Five white lines appeared.

"It is a letter," she said.

Ken looked closely.
"I don't know how to read it."

Mika brought paper and a pencil.
She put the paper on the stone and moved the pencil over it.
The white lines became dark on the paper.

Now they saw a map.
It showed the garden, the library window, and one small circle.

Taro pointed at the circle.
"There is nothing there."

They went to the circle.
There was only grass.

Ken remembered the stone.
"Maybe we must read it in the sun."

At four o'clock, the sun was low.
The stone's shadow pointed at the grass.
Mika put the paper on the ground.
The map line and the shadow line crossed.

Ken dug there with a small garden shovel.
He found a metal box.

Inside was a packet of flower seeds and a note:
"For the students who learn how to read the stone."

Mr. Sato came to the garden.
"That stone was made by the first gardening club," he said.

Yui held the seeds carefully.
"Then the message was not about treasure."

Ken smiled.
"It was about flowers waiting for readers."
