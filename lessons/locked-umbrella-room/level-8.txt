The Locked Umbrella Room

On the morning of the school festival, the umbrella room was locked from the inside.

That was impossible, because the umbrella room had no window, no back door, and no person inside it.

At least, that was what everyone said.

Mika looked at the locked door. Ken looked at the wet floor. Riku looked at the paper sign on the wall.

UMBRELLAS FOR GUESTS.
PLEASE TAKE ONE.

"The guests will come in thirty minutes," Mika said. "If this room stays locked, half of them will get wet."

Outside, rain hit the windows like small stones.

Mr. Sato came with a ring of keys. He tried one key, then another. None worked.

"This lock was changed yesterday," he said. "The new key was put in my desk."

"Was it stolen?" Riku asked.

"Maybe it was moved by mistake," Ken said.

Mika pointed to the floor. A thin line of water ran from the locked door to the hall. The line did not come from outside. It came from under the door.

"Something wet is inside," she said.

Riku put his ear to the door. "I hear a sound."

Everyone listened.

Tap. Tap. Tap.

It sounded like rain, but softer.

"There is a person inside," Riku whispered.

"Or an umbrella ghost," he added, because Riku could not stay serious for more than twelve seconds.

Ken looked at the door handle. A small string was tied around it. The string went under the door.

"This was tied from outside," he said.

"Then the room was not locked from inside," Mika said.

"But the key does not work," Mr. Sato said.

Ken followed the water line. It went to a small blue bucket by the stairs. The bucket was empty, but its bottom was wet. Beside it was a card.

The card said: FOR THE QUIET UMBRELLA.

Mika came down the stairs with a box of festival programs.

"Quiet umbrella?" she asked. "That sounds sad."

Mika checked the card. "The writing is small. A first-year student may have written it."

They found the first-year students near the entrance. One boy looked nervous. His name was Toma.

"I did not lock the room," Toma said quickly.

Again, no one had asked him yet.

Ken smiled a little. "What did you do?"

Toma looked at the floor. "Yesterday, I found an umbrella that was broken. It was open in the room, and it kept falling. I thought it was lonely, so I put it in the bucket."

"Why?" Riku asked.

"Because it was raining inside the room," Toma said.

That made no sense. Then it made too much sense.

Mika ran back to the door. "The sound is not a ghost. It is water."

Mr. Sato remembered something. "The room is under the science room sink."

Ken looked up. "A pipe?"

They ran upstairs. In the science room, a small pipe under the sink was loose. Water was dropping into the floor, then down into the umbrella room. The broken umbrella had opened under the leak, and the water was hitting it.

Tap. Tap. Tap.

"The lock was not the real problem," Mika said.

"Then what about the key?" Mr. Sato asked.

Mika opened the festival program box. Inside, under the papers, was a shiny new key.

"This box was carried from your desk this morning," she said. "The key was moved with it."

Mr. Sato looked embarrassed. "By me."

The umbrella room was opened by Mr. Sato. The wet broken umbrella was taken out by Ken and Toma. The floor was wiped by Riku, though he complained that a detective should not be used as a mop.

Mika put a new sign on the door:

THE UMBRELLAS ARE NOT HAUNTED.
THE PIPE WAS BROKEN.

Guests came in just as the rain became stronger. Everyone took umbrellas and smiled.

Toma looked at the broken umbrella. "What will happen to it?"

Mika tied a small ribbon to it. "It can be the festival sign. It already worked hard."

At the entrance, the broken umbrella was put in a stand with flowers. People thought it was decoration.

Riku bowed to it. "Thank you, quiet umbrella."

Ken laughed. Mika looked at the ceiling one more time.

"Next year," she said, "we check the pipes before we check the ghosts."
