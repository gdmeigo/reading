The Pudding Case

On Friday morning, the cooking room was locked. That was not strange. What was strange was the sign on the door.

THE CLASS PUDDING WAS MOVED BY A PERSON WITH GREAT POWER.

Taro read the sign twice. Jiro read it three times. Taro read only the last two words and looked impressed.

"Great power?" Taro said. "Maybe a superhero came."

"Or maybe Jaio came," Jiro said.

Everyone looked down the hall. Jaio was standing near the shoe boxes with his arms folded. Suneta was behind him with the same folded arms, but his arms were too thin to look strong.

Jaio had a spoon in his pocket. Suneta had two spoons.

"Don't look at me," Jaio said. "I did not eat the pudding."

No one had asked him yet.

The pudding was for the school open day. Ms. Kato had made it with the cooking club. It was famous.

"The pudding was put in the cold box yesterday," Jiro said. "The door was locked after that."

Taro looked at the lock. It was not broken. He looked at the floor. There were small yellow drops from the door to the stairs.

"A trail," he said.

Taro looked serious. "A pudding trail."

Jaio laughed. "A great detective needs a great assistant. Suneta, follow it."

Suneta bent down. "Why me?"

"Because you are closer to the floor," Jaio said.

Suneta sighed, but he followed the yellow drops. Taro, Jiro, and Jaio followed him.

The trail went to the music room. Inside, the piano was covered with a white cloth. On the cloth was a yellow mark shaped like a small hand.

"The pudding was touched here," Taro said.

"Maybe the pudding was carried here," Jiro said.

Taro lifted the piano cloth. There was no pudding under it. There was only a recorder, three music books, and a note.

The note said:

I was not eaten.
I was taken to a safer place.

"A safer place?" Taro said. "For a pudding?"

Suneta looked worried. "Maybe the pudding is in danger."

Jaio put his spoon back in his pocket. "A pudding is always in danger near students."

They heard a soft sound from the hall.

Tap. Tap. Tap.

It was not a footstep. It was a spoon hitting a cup.

They ran to the science room. The door was open a little. Inside, the big pudding was sitting on the teacher's desk.

But it was not alone. Six small cups were around it. A paper crown was on top of the pudding.

Taro whispered, "It became a king."

Jaio stepped forward. "Good. I will check if the king is safe."

Jiro stood in front of him. "Don't touch it."

"I am not touching it," Jaio said. "I am checking with my spoon."

"That is touching," Taro said.

Then the cupboard door opened. Hana came out with two first-year students.

"Sorry," Hana said. "We moved it."

"The pudding was moved by you?" Jiro asked.

Hana nodded. "Yes. The cold box was making a strange sound. We thought it was broken. So we moved the pudding here because this room is cool."

One first-year student pointed at Jaio. "We wrote the sign because he said yesterday, 'Only a person with great power can move that pudding.'"

Everyone looked at Jaio.

Jaio looked at the floor. "That was true. It is a very big pudding."

Suneta raised his hand. "I wrote 'great power.' But I did not know it would look like a crime."

Taro looked at the yellow drops. "What about the trail?"

Hana said, "That was our mistake. The pudding moved when we carried it. Some sauce fell."

"Then why did you hide in the cupboard?" Taro asked.

Hana looked embarrassed. "We heard Jaio say he would check the pudding with a spoon."

Suneta put the spoons on the desk. "For safety," he said.

Ms. Kato came in at that moment. She looked at the pudding. She looked at the crown. She looked at the spoons.

"Why is my pudding wearing a crown?" she asked.

No one answered.

Then Taro pointed at Jaio. "It was done by a person with great power."

Jaio opened his mouth, but Suneta spoke first.

"Actually," Suneta said, "it was done by a person with great fear."

Everyone laughed, even Jaio.

Ms. Kato did not laugh yet. She took the crown off the pudding.

"The pudding was moved safely," she said. "The sauce was dropped, but the pudding is fine."

Jaio stood very straight. "Then may I check it now?"

"Yes," Ms. Kato said.

Jaio smiled.

"With your eyes," Ms. Kato said.

Jaio stopped smiling.

At open day, the pudding was served by the cooking club. The story was told by Taro.

Jaio got the last cup because he had to help clean the yellow drops. Suneta helped him. He complained, but he cleaned carefully.

When the floor was clean, Ms. Kato gave them two small cups.

Jaio looked at his cup. "This is smaller than everyone else's."

Suneta looked at his own cup. "Mine is smaller than yours."

Taro smiled. "Maybe it was measured by justice."

Jaio took one bite and became quiet. Suneta took one bite and became quiet, too.

Taro leaned over. "What happened?"

Jaio looked at the empty cup.

"The pudding was moved again," he said.

"By whom?" Hana asked.

Jaio pointed at his own mouth.

"By a person with great speed."
