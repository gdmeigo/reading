The Quiet Bread Shop

Daichi and Emi walked home after school.
They passed a small bread shop near the station.
The shop smelled warm and sweet, but no one was inside.

An old man stood behind the table.
There were many fresh rolls in front of him.
He looked worried.

"Maybe people do not like my rolls now," he said.

Emi wanted to say something kind, but Daichi was looking at the door.
There was a sign on the glass door.
From inside, it said, "Open."

Daichi went outside and looked at the sign.
From the street, it said, "Closed."

"I think we found the problem," said Daichi.

Emi turned the sign around.
Then she opened the door a little, so people could smell the bread.

First, a woman came in.
Then two students came in.
Then a man from the station came in.

"I thought the shop was closed," said the woman.
"Me, too," said one student.

The old man looked at the sign and laughed.
"All morning, I was open only to myself."

Soon, almost all the rolls were gone.
The old man put two rolls in a paper bag.
"These are for the sign doctors," he said.

Daichi and Emi ate the rolls on the way home.
They were still warm.
