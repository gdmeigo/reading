The Quiet Bread Shop

Daichi and Emi walked home after school. Near the station, there
was a small bread shop. Usually, the shop was full of people, and
the street smelled warm and sweet. But that day, the shop was
quiet.

Daichi stopped at the window. There were many fresh rolls on the
table. Behind the table, an old man was standing with his arms
down. He looked at the bread and sighed.

"Maybe people do not like my rolls now," he said.

Emi looked at the rolls. They looked good. Daichi looked at the
door. There was a sign on the glass door. From inside the shop,
the sign said, "Open."

Daichi went outside and looked at the same sign from the street.
Then he laughed.

"What is funny?" asked Emi.

"From here, it says 'Closed,'" said Daichi.

The old man came to the door. He looked at the sign from the
street. His eyes got big.

"All morning," he said, "I was open only to myself."

Emi turned the sign around. Then she opened the door a little, so
the smell of the bread went out to the street.

First, a woman came in.
"Oh, you are open," she said. "I thought the shop was closed."

Then two students came in. Then a man from the station came in.
Soon, the quiet shop was not quiet at all.

The old man put rolls in bags. Daichi gave the bags to people. Emi
took coins and put them in a small box.

After twenty minutes, almost all the rolls were gone.

The old man put two last rolls in a paper bag.
"These are for the sign doctors," he said.

Daichi and Emi ate the rolls on the way home. They were still warm.

The next day, the sign on the door was bigger.
Under the word "Open," there was one more line:
"Please check both sides."
