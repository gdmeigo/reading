The Quiet Bread Shop

The quiet bread shop opened every morning at six, but nobody in town knew when the baker slept.

Aya noticed it first. She delivered newspapers before school, and every day she saw Mr. Mori putting warm bread in the window. He always smiled, but the smile was thin, as if he was saving the rest of it for someone who was not there.

One rainy morning, Aya found a paper bag outside the shop. On it was written: For the person who comes back.

Inside was one small roll, still warm.

Aya brought it to Mr. Mori. He held the bag for a long time.

"My daughter used to write that," he said. "She wanted to travel and become a cook. We argued before she left. I kept making her favorite bread, but she never came back."

Aya did not know what to say. She only asked, "May I help?"

After school, Aya and her friend Daichi made a small sign: TODAY'S BREAD HAS A STORY. They put it in the window. Under it, Mr. Mori wrote one sentence about the roll and his daughter.

The next day, many people came. Some bought bread. Some wrote stories of their own: a lost cat, a first game, a grandmother's birthday, a letter that was never sent.

On Saturday, a woman stood in front of the window for a long time. She had a travel bag and tired eyes.

"This bread," she said, "is it still for the person who comes back?"

Mr. Mori could not speak.

Aya quietly turned the sign around. On the back she had written one more sentence:

Today's bread is ready.
