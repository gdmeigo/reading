The Quiet Bread Shop

Five graded text-only readings.

Level 1

Assumed grading: present see/is/have/look/turn/come/smile, open/closed, simple nouns.

Ken sees a bread shop.

No one is in the shop.

Yui sees a sign.

The sign says, "Closed."

But the door is open.

An old man is in the shop.

He has many rolls.

Ken looks at the sign.

Yui turns the sign.

Now it says, "Open."

People come in.

The old man smiles.

Level 2

Assumed grading: Level 1 plus walked/was/were/looked/bought, soon, problem.

Ken and Yui walked by a bread shop.

The shop was quiet.

No one was in it.

There were many rolls on the table.

An old man was behind the table.

He looked sad.

Yui saw a sign on the door.

It said, "Closed."

"But the door is open," said Ken.

Yui turned the sign.

Now it said, "Open."

Soon, a woman came in.

Then two boys came in.

They bought rolls.

The old man smiled.

"Oh," he said. "That was the problem."

Level 3

Assumed grading: Level 2 plus smelled, sighed, outside/inside, quickly, learned.

Ken and Yui walked by a small bread shop.

The shop smelled good, but no one was inside.

On the table, there were many warm rolls.

An old man stood behind the table.

He looked at the rolls and sighed.

"No one wants bread today," he said.

Yui looked at the door.

There was a sign on it.

The sign said, "Closed."

"The shop is closed," said Yui.

"No, it is open," said the old man.

Ken opened the door and looked outside.

From the street, the sign said, "Closed."

From inside, it said, "Open."

Yui turned the sign around.

Five minutes later, a woman came in.

Then a man came in.

Then three students came in.

The rolls were gone very quickly.

The old man laughed.

"Today I learned something," he said.

"Good bread needs a good sign."

He gave Ken and Yui two small rolls.

Level 4

Assumed grading: Level 3 plus usually-like setting, worried, thought, all morning, sign doctors.

Ken and Yui walked home after school.

They passed a small bread shop near the station.

The shop smelled warm and sweet, but no one was inside.

An old man stood behind the table.

There were many fresh rolls in front of him.

He looked worried.

"Maybe people do not like my rolls now," he said.

Yui wanted to say something kind, but Ken was looking at the door.

There was a sign on the glass door.

From inside, it said, "Open."

Ken went outside and looked at the sign.

From the street, it said, "Closed."

"I think we found the problem," said Ken.

Yui turned the sign around.

Then she opened the door a little, so people could smell the bread.

First, a woman came in.

Then two students came in.

Then a man from the station came in.

"I thought the shop was closed," said the woman.

"Me, too," said one student.

The old man looked at the sign and laughed.

"All morning, I was open only to myself."

Soon, almost all the rolls were gone.

The old man put two rolls in a paper bag.

"These are for the sign doctors," he said.

Ken and Yui ate the rolls on the way home.

They were still warm.

Level 5

Assumed grading: Level 4 plus longer paragraphs, usually, both sides, helping in shop, final sign joke.

Ken and Yui walked home after school. Near the station, there was a small bread shop. Usually, the shop was full of people, and the street smelled warm and sweet. But that day, the shop was quiet.

Ken stopped at the window. There were many fresh rolls on the table. Behind the table, an old man was standing with his arms down. He looked at the bread and sighed.

"Maybe people do not like my rolls now," he said.

Yui looked at the rolls. They looked good. Ken looked at the door. There was a sign on the glass door. From inside the shop, the sign said, "Open."

Ken went outside and looked at the same sign from the street. Then he laughed.

"What is funny?" asked Yui.

"From here, it says 'Closed,'" said Ken.

The old man came to the door. He looked at the sign from the street. His eyes got big.

"All morning," he said, "I was open only to myself."

Yui turned the sign around. Then she opened the door a little, so the smell of the bread went out to the street.

First, a woman came in. "Oh, you are open," she said. "I thought the shop was closed."

Then two students came in. Then a man from the station came in. Soon, the quiet shop was not quiet at all.

The old man put rolls in bags. Ken gave the bags to people. Yui took coins and put them in a small box.

After twenty minutes, almost all the rolls were gone.

The old man put two last rolls in a paper bag. "These are for the sign doctors," he said.

Ken and Yui ate the rolls on the way home. They were still warm.

The next day, the sign on the door was bigger.

Under the word "Open," there was one more line: "Please check both sides."
