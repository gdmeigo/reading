The Quiet Bread Shop

Daichi and Emi walked by a bread shop.
The shop was quiet.
No one was in it.

Daichi had a book. He wanted to read it at lunch.
Emi had water. She wanted to drink it with bread.

There were many rolls on the table.
An old man was behind the table.
He looked sad.

Emi saw a sign on the door.
It said, "Closed."

"But the door is open," said Daichi.
Emi turned the sign.
Now it said, "Open."

Soon, a woman came in.
Then two boys came in.
They bought rolls.

Emi gave the old man a smile.
Daichi showed him the sign.

The old man smiled.
"Oh," he said. "I made good bread, but the sign said no."

He gave them two warm rolls.
"An open sign says welcome," he said.

More than half of the rolls were gone by lunch.
Daichi read his book in the shop.
Emi drank her water and smiled.
