The Wrong Robot

Sota's father made a small robot.
Its name was Robo-ji.
It wanted to help the family.

At first, Robo-ji took soup to Sota.
But it put the soup on Sota's math book.

"That is not lunch," said Sota.

"Don't be sad," said Mother.
"Let's fix it."

Then Mother said, "Clean the room, please."

Robo-ji picked up shoes, bags, and newspapers.
It put all of them in the bath.

"Why did you do that?" asked Sota.
"How did you get so many shoes?"

"The bath was empty," said Robo-ji.
"I used the bath because it was big," said Robo-ji.
"There was nothing in it. Now there is something in it."

Sota wrote a rule.
Ask before you move something.

Robo-ji read the rule.
"Maybe I understand," it said.

Then it asked about every cup in Grandma's box.
Sixty times.

"Come here, Robo-ji," said Sota.
"We need one more rule."
