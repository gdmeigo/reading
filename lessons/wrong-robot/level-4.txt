The Wrong Robot

Sota's father made a robot which was small, round, and very proud.
It had one button on its head.
The button said HELP.

"Call it Robo-ji," said Father.

Robo-ji opened its eyes and picked up a plate of soup.
Then it put the soup on Sota's math book.

"That is not helpful," said Sota.

"Maybe the book was hungry," said Robo-ji.

Mother asked Robo-ji to clean the living room.
Robo-ji picked up every shoe, bag, and newspaper.
It put all of them in the bathtub.

"Why?" asked Mother.

"The bathtub was empty," said Robo-ji.

Sota did not want Robo-ji to feel bad.
He wrote three rules.
Ask before you move something.
Food for people is not always food for dogs.
If you do not understand, say maybe.

Robo-ji read the rules.
"Maybe I am helpful now," it said.
