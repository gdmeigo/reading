The Last Seat at the Table

The lunch room had one problem.

There were thirty students in Class 2-B, but only twenty-nine seats had been set at the long table.

No one noticed at first. Everyone was too busy looking at the special curry. The curry was made by Ms. Kato and the cooking club for the last day of term. It smelled so good that even the quiet students began to talk.

Rina noticed the missing seat because Sota, her little brother, was visiting the class. He stood near the wall with his plate in both hands.

"Where do I sit?" he asked.

The room became quiet.

Ken stood up. "You can take my seat."

Yui stood up, too. "No, take mine."

Taro raised his hand. "I can eat standing."

Mika looked at him. "You spill water when you sit."

Taro sat down again.

Jaio, who had come to help carry plates, leaned against the door. "The last seat should be given to the strongest person."

Suneta stood behind him and nodded. "Yes. The strongest person. Or the strongest person's friend."

Rina looked at Sota. His face was small and tight. He hated being watched.

"This is not about strong people," she said.

Ms. Kato counted the seats. "I set thirty seats this morning."

"Then one was moved," Mika said.

Ken checked under the table. No seat. Yui checked near the wall. No seat. Taro checked behind the curry pot, which was not helpful but made him happy.

Then Sota pointed to the hallway. "There is one outside."

In the hall, a seat was standing beside the shoe boxes. On it was a note.

SAVE THIS FOR SOMEONE IMPORTANT.

Jaio folded his arms. "That sounds like me."

Suneta nodded. "Very important."

Mika took the note. "The seat was moved by someone who wanted to save it."

Rina saw Sota looking at the note. "Did you write this?"

Sota shook his head. "My letters are bigger."

Ken saw a small curry mark on one leg of the seat. "This was in the lunch room after the curry came."

Yui looked around. "Maybe someone was trying to be kind."

They asked the class. No one answered. Finally, a girl named Emi raised her hand.

"I moved it," she said quietly.

"Why?" Jaio asked. "Were you saving it for a king?"

Emi looked at Sota. "I was saving it for Ms. Kato."

Everyone turned to Ms. Kato. She was standing by the curry pot, still wearing her apron.

Emi's face turned red. "She always cooks. She always serves. She never eats with us. I wanted her to sit today."

The room went quiet again, but this time it was a warm quiet.

Ms. Kato put down the big spoon. "Thank you, Emi."

Sota looked at his plate. "Then I still have no seat."

Jaio sighed loudly. "Fine. I will stand."

Suneta stared at him. "Really?"

"Do not look so surprised," Jaio said. "A strong person can stand."

"A strong person can also carry one more seat," Mika said.

She pointed to the storage room.

Jaio and Suneta brought out a small folding seat. It was dusty, but it worked. The seat was wiped by Suneta. It was opened by Jaio with great drama, as if he had saved the school.

Sota sat down between Rina and Ms. Kato. Ms. Kato sat in the saved seat. Emi smiled for the first time that day.

When everyone began to eat, Taro raised his spoon. "Today we learned something."

"What?" Ken asked.

"Curry is better when nobody is left standing."

Ms. Kato laughed. "That is true."

After lunch, the dishes were washed by the class. The tables were cleaned by Ken and Yui. The folding seat was put back by Jaio and Suneta.

At the door, Sota pulled Rina's sleeve. "Was I someone important?"

Rina smiled. "Yes. But so was Ms. Kato."

Sota thought about that. Then he ran to Emi.

"Your note was good," he said.

Emi looked surprised. "Thank you."

Jaio heard him and pointed at the storage room. "Next time, write one more note: SAVE THIS FOR THE PERSON WHO FORGETS THE EXTRA SEAT."

Suneta nodded. "That person is also important."

Mika looked at Jaio. "That person is you."

Jaio picked up the folding seat again. "Then I am very important."

Everyone laughed, and this time no one had to stand alone.
