Family Photo Day

Nana's brother is six. His name is Kaito. He does not like photos.

"I am not a statue," Kaito says.

Nana's mother puts a blue shirt on the seat. "This is Kaito's shirt."

Kaito looks at the shirt. "It is too blue."

Nana's father laughs. "A shirt cannot be too blue."

Today is family photo day. Nana's grandmother is coming, too. The family wants one good photo for her room.

But Kaito runs to the garden. He hides behind a tree. Nana follows him.

"Why don't you like photos?" she asks.

Kaito looks at the ground. "In photos, everyone says, 'Smile.' Then my face feels strange."

Nana sits next to him. "Then don't make a photo smile. Make a Kaito smile."

"What is that?"

"A real smile," Nana says. "Think about something funny."

Kaito thinks. Then he says, "Father's socks."

Nana looks. Their father is at the door. One sock is black. One sock is white.

Kaito starts to laugh. Nana laughs, too. Their mother comes out and laughs. Their father looks down at his feet and laughs the loudest.

Grandmother arrives just then. She has a small camera.

"Wait," Nana says. "Now."

Grandmother takes the photo. Nobody is standing straight. Kaito is behind the tree. Father has two different socks. Mother is laughing with one hand on her face. Nana is pointing at the socks.

Later, Grandmother puts the photo in her room.

"This is my favorite family photo," she says.

Kaito looks at it. "My smile is not strange."

Nana says, "It is Kaito's smile."
