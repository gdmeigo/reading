Family Photo Day

Rina's brother is six. His name is Sota. He does not like photos.

"I am not a statue," Sota says.

Rina's mother puts a blue shirt on the seat. "This is Sota's shirt."

Sota looks at the shirt. "It is too blue."

Rina's father laughs. "A shirt cannot be too blue."

Today is family photo day. Rina's grandmother is coming, too. The family wants one good photo for her room.

But Sota runs to the garden. He hides behind a tree. Rina follows him.

"Why don't you like photos?" she asks.

Sota looks at the ground. "In photos, everyone says, 'Smile.' Then my face feels strange."

Rina sits next to him. "Then don't make a photo smile. Make a Sota smile."

"What is that?"

"A real smile," Rina says. "Think about something funny."

Sota thinks. Then he says, "Father's socks."

Rina looks. Their father is at the door. One sock is black. One sock is white.

Sota starts to laugh. Rina laughs, too. Their mother comes out and laughs. Their father looks down at his feet and laughs the loudest.

Grandmother arrives just then. She has a small camera.

"Wait," Rina says. "Now."

Grandmother takes the photo. Nobody is standing straight. Sota is behind the tree. Father has two different socks. Mother is laughing with one hand on her face. Rina is pointing at the socks.

Later, Grandmother puts the photo in her room.

"This is my favorite family photo," she says.

Sota looks at it. "My smile is not strange."

Rina says, "It is Sota's smile."
