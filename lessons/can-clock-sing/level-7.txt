Can the Clock Sing?

The clock in Room 2 was late every day. At nine o'clock, it said eight. At lunch, it said ten. Mr. Mori put a note under it: "Can this clock work?"

The next morning, the note was gone. In its place was a new note: "Can you listen?"

Ken read it twice. "A clock cannot write," he said.

Yui looked at the clock. "Can it sing?"

Ken laughed, but then the clock made a small sound. It was not a bell. It was a thin song, like someone singing far away.

The class became very quiet. Even the students near the door stopped moving. The song went up and down, but it did not sound happy.

"It sounds lost," Yui said.

Mr. Mori did not laugh. He took the clock down from the wall and put it on his desk. "If a clock can sing, maybe we should listen carefully," he said.

Mr. Mori opened the clock carefully. Inside, there was a tiny paper bird. The bird had a broken wing made of yellow paper.

"I can sing," the bird said, "but I cannot fly. When I sing, the clock forgets time."

Yui found tape. Ken found a piece of red paper. Together they made a new wing. The bird moved it once, then twice.

"Can you use this wing?" Yui asked.

"I can try," the bird said.

"Can you fly now?" Ken asked.

The bird flew around the room. The clock hands jumped to the right time.

At lunch, the clock rang once. A small note fell from it: "Thank you."

Mr. Mori looked at the class. "Can anyone explain this?"

Ken and Yui looked at each other. They could explain it, but only with a story no teacher could put on a test.

The paper bird landed on the clock again. This time it did not hide inside. It sat above the number twelve and sang one clear note.

Yui smiled. "Maybe the clock wanted music."

Mr. Mori nodded. "Then today, we can listen."
