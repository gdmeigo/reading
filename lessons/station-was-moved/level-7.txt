The Station Was Moved

Ken and Mika arrived at the town station at seven.
The morning train was not there.
The ticket gate was not there, either.

"The station was moved," Taro said.

Mika looked at the empty place. "A station cannot walk."

Then a silver sign appeared in the air.
It said, "Temporary space repair. Please wait."

Ken touched the sign, but his hand went through it.
Behind the sign, the old station slowly appeared like a picture on a screen.
People were inside it, but they were moving very slowly.

"Were they stopped?" Mika asked.

A small round robot came out of the ground.
"The station was borrowed by our ship," it said. "The train sound was needed by a lost satellite."

Taro opened his mouth. "You borrowed a whole station?"

"Only for six minutes," the robot said. "But human time was not checked."

Ken looked at the clock. It was still seven.

The station returned with a soft sound.
The ticket gate was opened by the station worker.
The train was seen at the end of the track.

The robot gave Mika a tiny silver ticket.
"This ticket was made by mistake," it said. "Please keep it."

Mika smiled. "Then the station was not lost."

Ken looked at the train.
"No," he said. "It just took a very short space trip."
